Food Handler Illness/Hygiene/Habits

Ensure that food-grade containers, emptied or not, are not to be stored on the floor at all times. Failure to do so may contaminate food. Ensure that vitamins are not to be stored in the kitchen to prevent possible chemical contamination.

Plastic Bins for foods were being stored beneath the work table. This was corrected at the time of the inspection. Bottle of Centrium Vitamins was found in kitchen. This was corrected at the time of inspection.

 3 3

Inadequate Cooling and/or Refrigerated Storage of Potentially Hazardous Food(s)

Ensure that chicken is properly cooled from cooking temperatures to 20 degrees C within 2 horus and then from 20 degrees C to 4 degrees C within 4 hours for refrigerated storage. If chicken is to be precooked prior to serving, ensure that they are adequately hot-held at 60 degrees C or greater at all times. Otherwise, ensure that chicken is not cooked in batches so that this infraction does not occur again. And ensure that chicken are cooked as they are ordered. Ensure that spring rolls are to be kept in refrigerated storage at 4 degrees C or less at all times and that they are taken out of the cooler when needed. Ensure that raw bean sprouts are properly stored in conditions of 4 degrees C or less at all times and protected from contamination using saran wrap.

Cooked chicken with a surface temperature of 55 degrees C was stored in the cooler. This was corrected at the time of the inspection. Spring rolls with an internal temperature of 12 degrees C were stored at room temperature. This was corrected at the time of the inspection. Raw bean sprouts with a surface temperature of 14 degrees C was stored at room temperature without adequate protection. This was corrected at the time of the inspection.

 3 3

Improper Cleaning, Sanitizing of Equipment and Utensils

Wiping cloths were not stored in a sanitizer solution. This was corrected at the time of the inspection.

Ensure that wiping clothes are to be stored in a 100 ppm bleach solution (1 oz. bleach per 1 gallon of water) at all times to faciliate proper sanitizing of food contact surfaces.

Food Not Protected - General

Various items in cooler found to be unprotected with food-grade covers or saran wrap. a. Cooked Pork in bowl b. Cooked Shrimp in bowl c. Cooked Shrimp in a basket d. Cabbage e. Pre-cooked Chicken Bowl of Pork and Shrimp were double-stacked All of these were corrected at the time of the inspection. Prep cooler inserts were not protected from contamination. This was corrected at the time of inspection.

Ensure that if food items are to be placed in coolers, they are placed in a manner that protects them from contamination such as using food-grade covers or saran wrap. Ensure that items stored on the prep-cooler are covered with a food-grade cover to prevent possible contamination.

Improper Sanitary Facilities

Ensure that hand-washing sinks are equipped with hot and cold running water, dispensable soap and single service paper towels at all times to facilitate proper hand-washing. Ensure that hand-washing sinks are free from clutter or any obstruction to facilitate proper hand-washing.

The two hand-washing sinks did not have dispensable paper towels. One was corrected at the time of the inspection. A laddle was placed in the hand-washing sink at the time of the inspection and was corrected.

 3 2

Improper Construction/Maintenance/Sanitation of Establishment

Deep fryer area and hard-to-reach areas on floors accumulated with filth and grease. No evidence of pest activity at the time of inspection. Chest freezer shows accumulation of food debris.

Ensure that floors are cleaned and sanitized on a regular basis to prevent food contamination and pest harbourage and for the purposes of pest monitoring. Ensure that chest freezer is cleaned out to prevent the contamination of food products.
