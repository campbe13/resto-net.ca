Lack of/Inadequate Food Safety Plan

Ensure a FOOD SAFETY PLAN is in place in the restaurant. The business is required by law to have an up to date Food Safety Plan that details how food is safely handled from receipt to sale or consumption. This plan must include the identification of critical control points (CCPs) and critical control limits (CCLs) which must be monitored daily and documented. The plan must describe the process followed to ensure adherence to the critical limits, and the actions to be taken in the event that the critical limits are not adhered to. The Food Premises Regulation (B.C. Reg. 210/99) section 23 requires that: "every operator of a food service establishment must have written procedures to ensure that a health hazard does not occur in the operation of the establishment."

Food Safety Plan monitoring AND recording of cooler temperatures and hot holding temperatures is now occurring.

Food Not Protected From Contamination - Microbiological

Cilantro was observed to be stored on the bottom shelf of a cart in the kitchen area. The cilantro was too close to the ground and too close to employee shoes and cloths as they walked past. Store the cilantro in an area that is more protected from potential contamination.

 1 0

Improper Cleaning, Sanitizing of Equipment and Utensils

The dishwasher and glass washer MUST be tested for adequate sanitizer concentration daily. The concentration must be recorded on a log sheet. All staff washing dishes must be aware of the process for testing for sanitizer concentration. They must always have easy access to sanitizer test kits that must be held in a dry location nearby.

The low temperature mechanical dishwasher was tested to be producing adequate chlorine sanitizing solution. Chlorine test strips were available to check the dishwasher for adequate sanitizer concentration. Staff present were not aware if the dishwasher and glasswasher sanitizer concentrations are being recorded daily.

 1 0

Improper Cleaning, Sanitizing of Equipment and Utensils

The sanitizer spray bottle in the downstairs kitchen area labelled as a Quaternary Ammonia had previously contained a powerful bleach solution, but is now full of quaternary ammonia solution as it should. Visibly dirty wiping cloths were observed to be held on food preparation tables in the downstairs kitchen. When not being used, wiping cloths should be rinsed and then held immersed in a bucket of sanitizing solution. Staff in the downstairs cooking area were not aware that this was a sanitizing solution and when they should be using it. Ensure that all staff are educated on the importance of sanitizing work surfaces to prevent cross contamination of food.

 1 0

Inadequate Insect/Rodent Control

The operator must comply with section 26 of the Food Premises Regulations: Every operator of food premises must ensure that the premises are (a) free of pests, (b) free of conditions that lead to the harbouring or breeding of pests, and (c) protected against the entrance of pests. WARNING: Failure to comply with Section 26 of the Food Premises Regulations may result in a violation ticket being issued ($230).

Live cockroaches were not observed in the staff washroom/storage room near the kitchen area. two dead cockroackes were observed on a sticky trap. A hole in the wall for plumbing access was observed below the sink in this room. Ensure that all cracks and crevices are pest proofed. Repair damage to walls in the dishwashing area. Eco lab had apparently treated for cockroaches recently.

 1 0

Improper Construction/Maintenance/Sanitation of Establishment

In the downstairs kitchen, the ice machine was not observed to be leaking water onto the floor, but the floor was wet.

Ensure that the ice machine is fixed or replaced so that it does not leak. Water on the floor will provide the conditions and moisture required by pests to thrive.

 1 0

Improper Construction/Maintenance/Sanitation of Establishment

In the downstairs kitchen, it was AGAIN observed that knives are being stored between a metal table and the tiled wall behind. The wall was observed to be visibly dirty. Hold all knives and utensils in a sanitary manner. I would recommend holding knives in a clean container or on a clean magnetic knife strip.

 1 0

Food Handler Training

Staff present at the time of the inspection were Foodsafe certified. The owner says that other staff have been signed up for Foodsafe classes to ensure that they always have at least one employee present with Foodsafe.

A minimum of 1 trained food handler must be present on-site at all times food is being handled or prepared. >Immediately register other food handlers so that this requirement can be met. See website at: http://www.foodsafe.ca The Food Premises Regulation (B.C. Reg. 210/99) section 10(1) states: "Every operator of a food service establishment must hold a certificate, issued by a health official, for the successful completion of the food handler training program known as FOODSAFE or its equivalent. 10(2) Every operator of a food service establishment must ensure that, while the operator is absent from the food service establishment, at least one employee present in the establishment holds the certificate referred to in subsection (1)." WARNING: Failure to comply with Section 10 of the Food Premises Regulation may result in a violation ticket being issued ($230).
