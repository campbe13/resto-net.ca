Lack of/Inadequate Food Safety Plan

Equip all cooling units with accurate thermometers and monitor/record temperatures of all coolers, hot holding, and the dishwasher final rinse temperature twice a day. Use the probe thermometer for checking internal temperatures of foods (i.e. hot holding, and cooking temperatures). Check sanitizer concentration throughout the day. Carry out any required corrective actions if necessary.

Some coolers were lacking thermometers. No temperature montitoring and recording is being carried out on a daily basis.

Lack of/Inadequate Food Safety Plan

Owner must provide a Food Safety Plan. This was required within 90 days of opening (Aug 5th, 2011) as per the conditons on the Operating Permit. Section 23 (2) of the BC Food Premise Regulation states that: Every operator of a food service establishment must have written procedures to ensure that a health hazard does not occur in the operation of the establishment. The written procedures required by subsection (2) must include: (a) identification of all critical control points, (b) critical limits for those critical control points, (c) the procedures to be followed to ensure adherence to the critical limits, and (d) the actions to be taken in the event that the critical limits are not adhered to.

Submit a written Food Safety Plan to the EHO within 2 weeks time. Resources provided.

Inadequate Cooling and/or Refrigerated Storage of Potentially Hazardous Food(s)

All food items were discarded. All potentially hazardous foods must be maintained at <4 degrees C or > 60 degrees C. Potentially hazardous foods that have been cooked (i.e. sushi rice) and are intended to be cooled prior to serving, are to be cooled from 60 C (140F) to 20 C (68F) or less within two hours and then from 20 C (68F) to 4 C (40F) or less within 4 hours. Put cooked rice in shallow pans into the cooler (at 4 C or less) asap for rapid cooling.

Potentially hazardous food temperature in excess of 4 degrees C. Potentially hazardous foods not stored in a manner that allows for quick cooling. 1. Sauce (oil, cut vegetables, garlic) was found on top of the microwave at ambient temperatures in a blender (it had been forgotten). Temperature measured 18.3 degrees C. 2. Sushi rice on counter at ambient temperatures and sushi rice in containers measured 20.4 degrees C (cooked 2.5hrs prior to the inspection) and 46.5 degrees C.

Improper Cleaning, Sanitizing of Equipment and Utensils

Wiping cloths used to clean and sanitize food contact surfaces not stored properly (e.g. sanitizing solution not used). Wiping cloths provide the optimal conditions for microbial growth. Using these cloths to wipe food contact surfaces without being stored in sanitizer may contaminate food.

Store dirty/wet wiping cloths in sanitizer solution when not in use to prevent microbial growth and thus, prevent contamination of food contact surfaces. Sanitizer solution can consist of 100ppm Cl or 200ppm quats in buckets. Refesh solution throughout the day to ensure concentration is adequate. Check concentration of sanitizer with test strips. Note: equip the premie with chlorine test strips (they can be purchased from any restaurant supply store).

 1 0

Improper Cleaning, Sanitizing of Equipment and Utensils

Equip the premise with a sanitizer (either a quats-based or diluted bleach solution) in a spray bottle and in a bucket for dirty wiping cloths and ensure the concentration is adequate enough for sanitizing.. Check the concentration with test strips (the concentration must be >200ppm for quats and >100ppm for chlorine in order to sanitize). Refresh solution as often as needed. Clean and sanitize surfaces and equipment throughout the day to maintain the premise in a sanitary manner. Minumum contact time for sanitizer is 2 minutes after washing.

Inadequate amount of approved sanitizer. Premise equipped with bleach but no sanitizer (i.e. bleach and diluted water).

 1 0

Improper Cleaning, Sanitizing of Equipment and Utensils

Food contact surfaces not washed, rinsed and sanitized after each use and following any times of operation when contamination may have occured. Food handlers admitted to washing and sanitizing food contact surfaces only a couple of times throughout the day despite using equipment and sufaces all throughout the day.

Ensure that all equipment and re-usable food contact surfaces (i.e. knives, cutting boards, counters, etc....) are washed, rinsed, and sanitized with a diluted bleach solution (minimum contact time of 2 minutes with 100ppm Cl) after each use to prevent contamination of foods.

Lack of/Inadequate Sanitation Plan

Devise and implement a Sanitation Plan with 30 days. Provide a copy of a written Sanitation Plan to the Environmental Health Officer via email, fax, or mail.

Owner/Operator does not have a written Sanitation Plan. As per Section 24 (1) of the BC Food Premise Regulation "Every operator of a food service establishment must have written procedures to ensure the safe and sanitary operation of the establishment. (2) The written procedures required by subsection (1) must include (a) the cleaning and sanitizing requirements for the establishment and for all equipment and utensils used in the establishment, (b) the identification of cleaning and sanitizing agents used in the establishment, including their concentrations and their uses, and (c) the identification of all pesticides used in the establishment, including their uses and their storage requirements".

Food Not Protected - General

1. Observed food containers stored on the floor (i.e. bag of rice stored on the floor and steamed rice in a container being stored on the floor). 2. Observed foods stored without protective covers (i.e. bag of open dry rice stored on the floor below a surface that had food debris (i.e. cooked rice).

1. All foods (and food in equipment) must be stored on shelving units off the floor (food storage is not permitted on the floor). 2. Put all open containers of food in containers with tight-fitting lids. Provide protective covers (i.e. plastic/stainless steel lids or plastic wrap) over foods to protect from contamination.

Improper Sanitary Facilities

1. 2-comp sink in the kitchen (used for kitchen staff hand washing) lacked hot water at the time of the inspection and single-use paper towels in a dispenser. The sink was also covered in an accumulation of food debris. 2. Handsink in front counter area not maintained in sanitary manner and used for other purpose other than hand washing. Handwashing is the single most effective method to prevent the transmission of any disease. Hand washing must include washing with warm water, liquid soap, rinsing and properly drying using papertowels. Providing this equipment ensures foodhandlers are able to properly wash their hands to prevent contamination of foods.

All handwashing stations must be fully equipped with hot/cold running water, liquid soap and papertowels in a dispenser. Hand sinks must be maintained in a sanitary manner and dedicated solely to hand washing (at lease one-compartment of the 2-comp sink). Train staff on proper handwashing procedures. This includes: frequency, the use of designated handsinks, and method. Note: the Owner must ensure that supply of hot and cold water is adequate in quantity and pressure to meet the water needs of the food premises at all times when in operation.

 2 0

Inadequate Insect/Rodent Control

Rodent droppings observed on the floor in the front sushi counter area.

The presence of rodents or insects must be treated immediately by: 1. Inspecting and discarding any adulterated food 2. Cleaning and sanitizing surfaces contaminated by pests (i.e. by using 1000ppm diluted bleach). 3. Cleaning and santizing areas with food debris and grease on a daily basis 4. Protecting the food premises against the entrance of pests - seal all gaps and holes. 5. Removing all unnecessary clutter. 6. Enlisting in the services of a pest control company if you do not have a contract. 7. A sanitation plan from the Operator will be required which will outline pest control measures that will be taken on a daily basis. Note: pest control companies are not responsible for daily monitoring, cleaning, sanitizing, and removal of pest activity. This is the Owner's responsibility.
