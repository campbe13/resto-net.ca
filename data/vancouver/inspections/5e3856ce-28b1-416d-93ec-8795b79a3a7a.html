Lack of/Inadequate Food Safety Plan

Outstanding - Owner must provide a Food Safety Plan. Section 23 (2) of the BC Food Premise Regulation states that: Every operator of a food service establishment must have written procedures to ensure that a health hazard does not occur in the operation of the establishment. The written procedures required by subsection (2) must include: (a) identification of all critical control points, (b) critical limits for those critical control points, (c) the procedures to be followed to ensure adherence to the critical limits, and (d) the actions to be taken in the event that the critical limits are not adhered to.

Submit a written Food Safety Plan to the EHO. Resources provided. Extension granted until Jan 2nd, 2012.

Inadequate Cooling and/or Refrigerated Storage of Potentially Hazardous Food(s)

Potentially hazardous foods (i.e. gyro meat, yogurt sauces, vegetables, cooked chicken pieces) are being kept in the cooler (at 4 C) and brought out of the cooler only when needed and put back in the cooler immediately after use. Refrain from storing potentially hazardous foods at ambient temperatures. Store all potentially hazardous foods in coolers at 4 degrees C or less at all times. If foods are stored in the dangerzone, then a maximum of 2 hours is permitted and then foods must be discarded (note: a timing system then will have to be implemented).

Inadequate Cooling and/or Refrigerated Storage of Potentially Hazardous Food(s)

Potentially hazardous foods such as salads prepared from potatoes or macaroni measured 4 degrees C in insert in prep cooler. Ensure that potentially hazardous foods that have been cooked and are intended to be kept under refrigerated storage prior to serving, are to be cooled from 60 C (140F) to 20 C (68F) or less within two hours and then from 20 C (68F) to 4 oC (40F) or less within 4 hours prior to putting in buffet (prep cooler inserts). Instructed staff to immediately place pasta in the cooler underneath to cool down properly prior to placing in inserts.

Inadequate Hot Holding of Potentially Hazardous Food(s)

Potentially hazardous hot food not being maintained above 60 degrees C. Observed chicken thighs stored piled above the level of the insert of the hot hold table measured 40.2 degrees C. Staff had turned off the unit to allow the potentially hazardous foods to cool down at ambient temperatures.

Maintain potentially hazardous foods at >60 C (or <4 C) at all times. Instructed staff member to put potentially hazardous foods in shallow pans and put into the cooler (at 4 C) as soon as possible for rapid cooling. Proper cooling requires removing heat from food quickly enough to prevent microbial growth. Excessive time for cooling of potentially hazardous foods has been consistently identified as one of the leading contributing factors to foodborne illness. Ensure that potentially hazardous foods that have been cooked and are intended to be kept under refrigerated storage prior to re-heating again at later date, are to be cooled from 60 C (140F) to 20 C (68F) or less within two hours and then from 20 C (68F) to 4 oC (40F) or less within 4 hours.

Improper Cleaning, Sanitizing of Equipment and Utensils

Outstanding/re-occurring observation - wiping cloths used to clean and sanitize food contact surfaces were again not stored properly (e.g. sanitizing solution not used). Wiping cloths stored in dirty water, concentration of santizer measured 0ppm Cl (it required to be refreshed). Wiping cloths provide the optimal conditions for microbial growth. Using these cloths to wipe food contact surfaces without being stored in sanitizer may contaminate food.

Store dirty/wet wiping cloths in sanitizer solution when not in use to prevent microbial growth and thus, prevent contamination of food contact surfaces. Sanitizer solution can consist of 100ppm Cl or 200ppm quats in buckets. Refesh solution throughout the day to ensure concentration is adequate. In addition, equip the premise with an approved sanitizer in a container (i.e. spray bottle) filled with either >100ppm chlorine (i.e. 1 tsp bleach per litre of water) or >200ppm quaternary ammonium and have available at all times when in operation and use throughout the day to prevent contamination of foods and maintain the premise in a sanitary manner.
