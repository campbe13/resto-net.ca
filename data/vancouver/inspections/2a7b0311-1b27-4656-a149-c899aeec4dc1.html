Inadequate Cooling and/or Refrigerated Storage of Potentially Hazardous Food(s)

Cooked chicken wings (43 C) and chick peas Masala (40 C) stored on a prep counter at room temperature. Corrected at time of inspection: Chicken was spread out on a baking sheet; chick peas Masala put into a s/s pan and both refirgerated.

After cooking, hot food must be kept hot in a hot hold unit OR cooled rapidly through the danger zone to 4 - 0 C. -Place the food in pre-chilled shallow pans (3-4 inches deep) and refrigerate immediately. -Store uncovered on the top shelf or cover loosely (allowing heat to escape). Storing potentially hazardous food at danger zone temperatures is not acceptable and must stop, effective immediately. Be advised that any future violations may result in food seizure and or issuing of a Violation Ticket and $345 Fine.

Inadequate Insect/Rodent Control

Mouse droppings were scattered on the floor underneath food equipment, shelves and along the perimeter.

Verbal directive issued to Sandeep to clean & disinfect the premises after closing tonight. >Remove droppings & urine and sanitize all contaminated surfaces using a bleach & water sanitizing solution. Prepare a fresh solution of sanitizer by mixing 1 ounce of chlorine bleach per gallon of water. >Discard any food items contaminated by mouse droppings or urine. >Seal holes & gaps, greater than 1/4 inch, through which mice may enter; Use wire mesh, galvanized metal or cement. >Hire a licensed pest control company to provide integrated mouse control; -Provide a copy of the work order/ invoice as proof of treatment. Be advised that failure to keep the premises free of pest may result in issuing a ViolationTicket and $230 Fine.
