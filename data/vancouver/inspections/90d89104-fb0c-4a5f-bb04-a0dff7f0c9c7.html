Lack of/Inadequate Food Safety Plan

A Food Safety Plan is still not in place at the restaurant as required. Ensure that a Food Safety Plan is in place in the restaurant. Prepare a Food Safety Plan for Sukiyaki including information on how the raw eggs are held and then used. Ensure that all coolers are supplied with accurate thermometers. If you require information on how to prepare a Food Safety Plan, I will be happy to provide written and verbal instructions.

The business is required by law to have an up to date Food Safety Plan that details how food is safely handled from receipt to sale or consumption. This plan must include the identification of critical control points (CCPs) and critical control limits (CCLs) which must be monitored daily and documented. The plan must describe the process followed to ensure adherence to the critical limits, and the actions to be taken in the event that the critical limits are not adhered to. The Food Premises Regulation (B.C. Reg. 210/99) section 23 requires that: "every operator of a food service establishment must have written procedures to ensure that a health hazard does not occur in the operation of the establishment." WARNING: Failure to comply with section 23 may result in a violaton ticket being issed.

Improper Cleaning, Sanitizing of Equipment and Utensils

The low temperature mechanical dishwasher was producing a 50 ppm chlorine sanitizing solution. AGAIN, no chlorine testing kit was available.

Ensure that the dishwasher is tested before use daily to ensure that adequate sanitizing solution is being produced. Staff must inspect the sanitizer bucket to ensure there is enough sanitizer left to last the shift. A chlorine test kit must always be available, and all staff must be aware of where it is and how to use it. Results of the daily sanitizer check must be recorded on a log sheet. Operator must ensure compliance with Section 17 of the B.C. Food Premises Regulations: 17 (1) Every operator of food premises must ensure that the premises and the equipment and utensils used on the premises are maintained in a sanitary condition. (2) Without limiting subsection (1), every operator of food premises must ensure that the equipment, utensils and food contact surfaces used on the premises are washed and sanitized in a manner that removes contamination. Lack of/Inadequate Sanitation Plan

Section 24 of the BC Food Premises Regulation (B.C. Reg. 210/99) states: 24 (1) Every operator of a food service establishment must have written procedures to ensure the safe and sanitary operation of the establishment. (2) The written procedures required by subsection (1) must include: (a) the cleaning and sanitizing requirements for the establishment and for all equipment and utensils used in the establishment, (b) the identification of cleaning and sanitizing agents used in the establishment, including their concentrations and their uses.

The restaurant is required to have a Sanitiation plan that describes how the restaurant is kept in a clean and sanitary condition. The plan must describe how, and when all areas and surfaces are cleaned and sanitized. The plan must detail how the dishwasher and glass washer work and what chemicals are used to sanitize dishes in these machines, at what concentration and how the concentration is determined (appropriate chemical test kits). I would also like you to include a section on pest control in the restaurant. The plan must be submitted to your inspector for approval.
