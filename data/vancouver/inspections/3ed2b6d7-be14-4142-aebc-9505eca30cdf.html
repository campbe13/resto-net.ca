Inadequate Cooling and/or Refrigerated Storage of Potentially Hazardous Food(s)

Ensure all potentially hazardous foods are properly cooled (from 60 degrees C to 21 degrees C in two hours and from 20 degrees C to 4 degrees C in 4 hours) to prevent the growth of pathogens. Options include cooking smaller batches to allow for quicker cooling, using an ice bath or an ice wand.

Observed pot of curry chicken in the walk-in cooler to have an internal temperature of 9.2 degrees C using a probe thermometer. Operator stated that the delivery from the warehouse (Curry Den) was made yesterday and that half a bucket of the curry chicken was cooked. The staff who cooked the curry chicken was not present at time of inspection to confirm how the curry chicken was cooled prior to placing into the walk-in cooler. Other foods in the cooler were at 4 degrees C or degrees C or less. The curry chicken was discarded at time of inspection.

Improper Sanitary Facilities

Observed used cloth placed in handwashing station at the front by the hot-holding line. The cloth was removed at time of inspection.

Ensure that the handwashing station is clear of food/equipment at all times to allow for proper handwashing. Cloths placed in the handsink can be come contaminated. Cloths not in use should be placed in a 100 ppm chlorine or 200 ppm quaternary ammonium sanitizing solution.
