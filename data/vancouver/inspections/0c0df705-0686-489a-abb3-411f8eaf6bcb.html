Lack of/Inadequate Food Safety Plan

A food safety plan must be on-site at all times. All staff must be familiar with the contents and the location of the plan. If the food safety plan cannot be located, a new plan will have to be developed and submitted to Vancouver Coastal Health for approval.

 1 0

Inadequate Cooling and/or Refrigerated Storage of Potentially Hazardous Food(s)

The front glass cooler and the under-counter cooler were both too warm (10 degrees C). Cooked vegetables and hydrated rice noodles were being stored at room temperature. Meat was being stored "on" ice. This is not acceptable. Immediate corrective action is required. Potentially hazardous foods must be maintained at 4 degrees C or colder at all times. The front coolers may not be used for the storage of potentially hazardous foods until they are capable of maintaining a temperature of 4 degrees C or colder at all times. Each refrigeration unit must be equipped with a thermometer and temperatures must be monitored and recorded throughout the day. Potentially hazardous foods may not be stored on the counter at room temperature during busy lunch and dinner periods, nor may they be placed "on" ice. When using ice to store foods, the foods must be deeply submerged in the ice and the food temperatures must be monitored to ensure that they are at 4 degrees C or colder.

 4 2

Inadequate Hot Holding of Potentially Hazardous Food(s)

Noodles, cabbage, roll dips, chicken basil, etc. in the front hot-holding unit were not sufficiently hot (45-54 degrees C). This is unacceptable. Potentially hazardous foods must be maintained at 60 degrees C or hotter at all times. Temperatures must be monitored and recorded throughout the day. Should food temperatures fall below 60 degrees, the foods must be re-heated to an internal temperature of at least 74 degrees C. This may only be done once. Should food temperatures fall below 60 degrees C or second time, the foods must be discarded. Do not place foods in the hot holding unit until the unit has been sufficiently pre-heated.

 1 0

Improper Cleaning, Sanitizing of Equipment and Utensils

Rice scoops were being stored in water at room temperature. Rice scoops and utensils must be cleaned and sanitized after each use. Alternatively, they must be stored in a manner that precludes bacterial growth - either at 4 degrees C or colder or 60 degrees C or hotter. Immediate corrective action is required. Second Warning.

 1 0

Food Not Protected - General

No thawing of foods or food preparation may take place in the storage locker. Frozen foods must be thawed in a cooler, in a microwave oven or under cold, running water.

 1 1

Improper Sanitary Facilities

A plastic bag was in the hand wash sink. Please ensure that the hand sink is clear and accessible at all times.

 1 0

Inadequate Insect/Rodent Control

Mouse droppings were noted both in the restaurant, as well as in the storage locker. Required Action: All rodent excrerment must be removed from the floor and shelves, as well as under equipment. Use a flashlight to illuminate dark areas when cleaning. Notify your pest control representative of on-going rodent activity. All goods must be stored in pest-proof containers equipped with tight-fitting lids. All food-contact surfaces must be cleaned and sanitized at the start of each day to remove possible contamination left behind by rodents. Shelves in the storage locker must be thoroughly cleaned to remove accumulated droppings and rodent urine. Ensure that your pest control representative has access to your storage locker and that pest control services are provided in this area. Please refer to the information provided regarding the control of mice in food establishments. Second Warning.

 1 0

Improper Construction/Maintenance/Sanitation of Establishment

All over-turned dairy crates are to be removed and replaced with proper shelves which are smooth, washable and non-absorbent. Goods must be stored at lest 6 inches (15 cm) up off of the floor. Second Warning. The food preparation, food service and dry storage areas all require thorough cleaning, especially in hard-to-reach places such as under, behind and between equipment. Ensure that you use a flashlight when cleaning to help illuminate dark, hard-to-see areas. Ensure that clutter is required is reduced and that all areas are readily accessible.

Written documentation must be on-site with respect to the percentage of trans fat in your menu items.
