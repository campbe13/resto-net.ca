Lack of/Inadequate Food Safety Plan

A Food Safety Plan is due as soon as possible and within 90 days. Cooler temperatures are not being recorded. Cooler temperatures must be monitored AND recorded daily.

Improper Cleaning, Sanitizing of Equipment and Utensils

No sanitizing solution was available at the time of the inspection. >Ensure a sanitizer solution is available at all times in a bucket or spray bottle for sanitizing food contact surfaces. >To prepare a 100 ppm bleach sanitizing solution add 1/2 teaspoon of bleach to every 1 liter of water used. >Food contact surfaces must be properly washed, rinsed, and sanitized after each use. >Cutting boards (or other food contact surfaces) must be cleaned and sanitized (using the bleach solution or another approved sanitizer) after use. >Clean and sanitize cutting boards or other equipment at least every four hours when they are under the same continuous use. Hold wiping cloths fully immersed in a bucket of sanitizing solution when not being actively used.

Operator must ensure compliance with Section 17 of the B.C. Food Premises Regulations: 17 (1) Every operator of food premises must ensure that the premises and the equipment and utensils used on the premises are maintained in a sanitary condition. (2) Without limiting subsection (1), every operator of food premises must ensure that the equipment, utensils and food contact surfaces used on the premises are washed and sanitized in a manner that removes contamination.

 0 0

Improper Cleaning, Sanitizing of Equipment and Utensils

The cutting boards for the sushi area were observed to be standing on end on the floor in the sushi area. Ensure that the cutting boards are never in contact with dirty areas such as the restaurant floor. After cleaning and sanitizing the cutting boards must be placed back on the counters of the sushi area, or held in another clean and sanitary location.

 1 0

Food Not Protected - General

Bags of rice were observed to be stored in the furnace/boiler room directly on the floor. All food must be stored in a clean and sanitary location off the floor.

 1 0

Improper Sanitary Facilities

Ensure compliance with Section 21 of the Food Premises Regulations: 21(3) Every operator of food premises must ensure that each employee washes his or her hands as often as necessary to prevent the contamination of food. 21(4) Every operator of food premises must supply and maintain handwashing stations adequate in number and location to ensure convenient access to all employees

Access to the kitchen hand washing sink was partially blocked by a un-used cooler and a garbage can stored in front of the sink. There was no paper towel supplied at the sink. An employee pointed ot a cloth towel hanging at this sink when I asked how they dried their hands. The practice of regular hand washing is the first line of defense against microbial cross contamination and as such is considered extremely important. Hand washing must be undertaken between food handling and other tasks such as the handling of money, using the washroom and handling dirty dish ware. Ensure that all hand washing stations are supplied with liquid hand soap in pump style dispensers and paper towel,
