In Compliance - Food

High temperature dishwasher in the kitchen area was tested at 78.3 C and the bar high temperature dishwasher was tested as 74.4 C.

Temperature log sheet is in use & up to date

Freezer temperatures are satisfactory (< -18 degrees C / 0 degrees F).

Mechanical dishwashing procedure is satisfactory (final rinse water temperature is > 71 degrees C / 160 degrees F for 10 seconds, measured at the dish; or >82 degrees C/179 degrees F, measured at the manifold).

Handwashing facilities are satisfactory.

Equipment, utensils and food contact surfaces used on the premises are washed and sanitized in a manner that removes contamination.

 0 0

Inadequate Cooling and/or Refrigerated Storage of Potentially Hazardous Food(s)

Front north side preparation cooler was testing at 9-11 C. Ensure this cooler is operating at 4 C and below. Thermometer was present and temperatures are monitored daily, however it is important that all coolers meet the minimum requirement of 4 C. All coolers were covered with trays in order to maintain the internal temperature. Reduce the level of the potentially hazardous foods.

 1 0

Improper Cleaning, Sanitizing of Equipment and Utensils

Quaternary ammonium sanitizer was tested in the kitchen area, it was very high, approximately 800-900 ppm. This must be diluted with water to 200 ppm. Use the following steps: 1. use a minimum of 200 ppm of quaternary ammonium 2. place into a spray bottle, labelled as sanitizer or immerse all dishcloths into this solution 3. use throughout the daytime and utilize on all surfaces, utensils and equipment.

 1 0

Poor Sanitary Design, Construction, Installation of Equipment and Utensils

Observed a number of sinks leaking, including the kitchen dishwasher. The back two compartment sink and the front handsinks were leaking, these must be fixed immediately. The gaskets and seals on the front bar coolers were not operational. Ensure these areas are repaired. Observed areas thoroughout the kitchen were the walls require sealing and additional caulking. Ensure all surfaces are non-absorbent and easily cleanable. The upstairs general storage area requires further decluttering and reorganization. Remove all unnecessary items from this area. This area could potentially lead to rodent harborage and there is an overabundance of items stored here, which do not apply directly for the use of the facility. The staff storage area was also very cluttered and there was a number of personal items stored here, these items must be removed immediately. The outside storage area also requires thorough cleaning and remove all equipment pieces, that are not functional and operational.

 6 0

Improper Construction/Maintenance/Sanitation of Establishment

Clean the ceiling in the walk-in cooler. Observed some accumulation of dried food products and debris behind equipment, ensure all hard to reach areas are cleaned, especially in behind deep freezers and under the stainless steel equipment. Observed accumulation of grease and debris under the fryer and front grill area. Ensure these areas are thoroughly cleaned and sanitized. Areas of the floor require refinishing, this is not a tiled floor. It must be constructed of easily cleanable and non-absorbent surfaces. Thoroughly clean the hard to reach areas in the bar, again remove all unnecessary items from this area. Clean the backsplash, this is constructed from copper and is difficult to maintain, ensure this is cleaned adequately.

 6 0

Food Handler Training

No food handlers on-site at time of inspection have completed Foodsafe. A minimum of 1 trained food handler must be present on-site at all times food is being handled or prepared. Immediately register other food handlers so that this requirement can be met. See website at: http://www.foodsafe.ca Fax written confirmation of registration to inspector within one week.

Foodhandler was not trained with Foodsafe level 1 or its equivalence. Minimum requirement is one employee must be available with Foodsafe level 1 or equivalent.
