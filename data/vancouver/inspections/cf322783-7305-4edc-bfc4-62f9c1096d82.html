B. G. Urban Cafe

742 Granville
Vancouver BC V6Z 1E4
Primary Owner: Ideal Garden Cafe Ltd
Primary Operator: Irene Woo
Capacity: 0 Service Type: Restaurant - Full Service
Facility Web Site:

Inspection Details

Inspection Date: 12-Jul-2011
Inspector:
Follow-up Required: False
Reason for Inspection: Re-inspection
Action Taken: Verbal Directive
Referrals:
General Comments: Hand-delivered inspection report to operator. The refrigeration units were all still too warm (8 - 10 degrees). The operator was questioned why these refrigeration units were still in use. The operator stated that she plans on purchasing a new cooler, but that she needs some time. The operator was advised that, until the refrigeration units are capable of maintaining a temperature of 4 degrees C or colder, they cannot be used for the storage of potentially hazardous foods - not even over busy periods. Immediate corrective action is required.
Closing Comments:

Inspection Information

Observations # of New Observations # of Resolved Observations

Lack of/Inadequate Food Safety Plan

A written food safety plan is still not in place. Please refer to the information provided. A written food safety plan must be developed and forwarded to Vancouver Coastal Health for approval before September 01, 2011.

 1 0

Food Not Protected From Contamination - Microbiological

Unprotected muffins and croissants were being stored on the front counter. This is not acceptable. Baked goods must be protected from contamination at all times. They must be individually wrapped or placed behind a sneeze guard. Second Warning. Spoons used to dispense steamed milk were being stored in a container of water at room temperature. This is not a safe food handling practice. Spoons must be washed and sanitized after use or must be stored in a manner that precludes bacterial growth (either at 4 degrees C or colder or 60 degrees C or hotter). Immediate corrective action is required.

 3 1

Inadequate Cooling and/or Refrigerated Storage of Potentially Hazardous Food(s)

The under-counter glass refrigeration units in the front service area were again too warm. The juice cooler measured 10 degrees C and the dairy cooler measured 12.5 degrees C. Also, the right side of the refrigerated display case measured 8.7 degrees C. This is unacceptable. Immediate corrective action is required. Potentially hazardous foods must be maintained at 4 degrees C or colder at all times. Refrigeration temperatures must be monitored very closely and must be recorded in a logbook. The under-counter glass refrigeration units may not be used for the storage of potentially hazardous foods unless a temperature of 4 degrees C or colder can be maintained. Future violations may result in the issuance of a violation ticket. Second Warning.

 3 0

Improper Cleaning, Sanitizing of Equipment and Utensils

Chlorine test strips have still not been acquired. Chorine test strips must be acquired immediately to enable daily testing of the dishwasher's sanitizer concentration. Second Warning. The solution containing quaternary ammonium was much too strong. The solution must be diluted to 200 ppm quaternary ammonium. Quat test strips are to be provided to enable testing of your quat sanitizer concentration. Second Waning.

 2 0

Inadequate Insect/Rodent Control

Mouse droppings were noted under the dishwash counter and under storage shelves. All rodent excrerment must be removed. Rodent abatement strategies must be increased. Rodent activity must be monitored very closely. Second Warning.

 1 0

Improper Construction/Maintenance/Sanitation of Establishment

Improved cleaning of the floor is required to remove accumulated grime - especially along equipment and counters. The ice machine is leaking and must be repaired.

 5 3

TF: NO Trans Fat documentation for food is kept on site or provided on request

Please ensure that there is written documentation with respect to the percentage of trans fat in your menu items.
