Inspection Details

Inspection Date: 18-Jan-2011
Inspector:
Follow-up Required: True
Reason for Inspection: Re-inspection
Action Taken: Information Exchanged, Verbal Directive, Written Directive
Referrals:
General Comments: Routine follow-up conducted mid-afternoon. Accompanied by staff, Andrew and Angela. Previous deficiencies are still outstanding. - lack of thermometer in cooler and monitoring - time/temperature of tapioca balls at ambient temperatures is not being recorded. Spoke to staff member and it takes an hour to an hour and a half to make a fresh batch. The batch they had at ambient temperature had almost been sitting at ambient temperature for 2 hrs (temperature 25-26 degrees C) and yet the staff hadn't even started to make a new batch of tapioca balls. - a sanitizer solution was not available. Dirty wiping cloths were sitting on the counter. Instructed staff member how to prepare a diluted bleach solution for storing wiping cloths and to use on food contact surfaces throughout the day to prevent contamination of foods and maintain the premise in a sanitary condition. Received resistance from the staff member and did not want to store wiping cloths in diluted bleach or prepare any for use because the Owner has not instructed them to do so. Throughout the day they just use soap on surfaces and for wiping cloths and they just use diluted bleach on surfaces and the floor in the morning and at night. Dirty and wet wiping cloths can act as a reservior for pathogens (bacteria, viruses, etc..) which are not destroyed by using soap. - a few staff members may be registered for the Foodsafe course.

Observations # of New Observations # of Resolved Observations

Lack of/Inadequate Food Safety Plan

OUTSTANDING - Food Safety Plan Monitoring is not occurring. Kenmore cooler lacks a thermometer.

Equip all cooling units with accurate thermometers and monitor/record temperatures twice daily. Temperature log sheets provided.

 1 0

Food/Water Adulterated or Received From Unapproved Sources

OUTSTANDING - EHO will require proof of approved food source for tapioca balls. Staff claim that the product is purchased from the Metrotown area and transported to the Little Tea Shop daily by the Owner. The EHO will also need to be provided with details on how the food is transported safely (i.e. temperature control and prevention of contamination).

 1 0

Inadequate Cooling and/or Refrigerated Storage of Potentially Hazardous Food(s)

OUTSTANDING - Potentially hazardous food temperature in excess of 4 degrees C. Tapioca balls for bubble tea being held at ambient temperatures. Tapioca balls are a potentially hazardous food (i.e. capable of supporting the growth of pathogenic microorganisms) and must be kept out of the dangerzone (between 4 and 60 degrees C) otherwise discarded after 2hrs. Spoke with staff and the tapioca balls are delivered daily (in the am) and are kept at ambient temperature all day until used up and then any leftovers are discarded.

Owner/Operator must ensure that all potentially hazardous foods are stored at 4 degrees C or less or 60 degrees C or greater at all times. Otherwise, after 2 hours discard. Therefore, Owner must keep small batches in the cooler and bring out as the other is discarded and/or make own batches of balls frequently throughout the day.

 1 0

Improper Cleaning, Sanitizing of Equipment and Utensils

CORRECTED AT THE TIME OF INSPECTION - Lack of approved sanitizer. Premise must be equipped with a sanitizer that is readily available for use during operation. Use the sanitizer on all food contact surfaces and equipment to prevent contamination of foods and maintain the premise in a sanitary manner.

 1 1

Improper Cleaning, Sanitizing of Equipment and Utensils

Staff claim they now are using diluted bleach to sanitize dishes and utensils. Note: The proper procedure for manual dishwashing consists of the 3-compartment sink method: 1. Wash with warm water and soap 2. Rinse with warm water 3. Sanitize using 1 Tbsp bleach per gallon of water. Soak dishes and utensils for 2 minutes. Follow by air drying. Instructions provided to staff. Signage to follow.

 1 1

Improper Construction/Maintenance/Sanitation of Establishment

Conduct a thorough clean with special attention to hard to reach locations.

OUTSTANDING - Accumulation of food debris noted under some of the equipment and food spillage down the front of the sink cabinet.

 1 0

Food Handler Training

Owner must ensure that at least one person per shift is present with the Foodsafe Certificate Level 1 or equivalent. If staff members need training then have them get training within 30 days. Register at www.foodsafe.ca

OUTSTANDING - Both staff members present at the time of inspection do not have a Foodsafe Certificate Level 1 or equivalent. As per Section 10 of the BC Food Premise: 10 (1) Every operator of a food service establishment must hold a certificate, issued by a health official, for the successful completion of the food handler training program known as FOODSAFE or its equivalent. (2) Every operator of a food service establishment must ensure that, while the operator is absent from the food service establishment, at least one employee present in the establishment holds the certificate referred to in subsection (1).
