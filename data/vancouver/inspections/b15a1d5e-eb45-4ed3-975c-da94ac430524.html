Lack of/Inadequate Food Safety Plan

A written food safety plan must be developed. See information provided.

Food Not Protected From Contamination - Microbiological

Utensils were being stored in containers of water held at room temperature. This is not acceptable. Utensils must be cleaned and sanitized after use or must be stored in a manner that precludes bacterial growth (either at 4 degrees C or colder or 60 degrees C or hotter). Sugar was being stored in containers that were previously used for the storage of chemicals. This is not acceptable. Foods may only be stored in food-grade containers. Immediate corrective action is required.

 2 0

Inadequate Cooling and/or Refrigerated Storage of Potentially Hazardous Food(s)

The temperature of the bar cooler was 8.6 degrees C. This is not acceptable. Potentially hazardous foods must be maintained at 4 degrees C or colder at all times. Dairy products may not be stored in this cooler until it is capable of maintaining a safe temperature. Immediate corrective action is required. Temperature monitoring must take place throughout he day, especially during or immediately after peak demand periods so that you can determine the effectiveness of your refrigeration systems. Results are to be recorded in a log book.

 1 0

Improper Cleaning, Sanitizing of Equipment and Utensils

Please ensure that the dishwasher's chlorine sanitizer concentration is tested daily. At lest 50 ppm chlorine must be present in the final rinse cycle. Results are to be recorded in a log book. The slicer must be cleaned and sanitized after each use. Please refer to the guideline provided re: cleaning of slicers.

 2 0

Lack of/Inadequate Sanitation Plan

A written sanitation plan must be developed.

 1 0

Inadequate Insect/Rodent Control

A screen must be provided for the open window in the kitchen. Mouse droppings were noted along the kitchen walls and behind the upright freezers. All rodent excrerment must be removed. Please refer to the information provided re: rodent control in food establishments. All food contact surfaces must be cleaned and sanitized with bleach at the start of every day to remove possible contamination left behind by rodents.

 2 0

Improper Construction/Maintenance/Sanitation of Establishment

The wooden base under the mixer must be re-finished so that it is smooth, washable and non-absorbent. The lighting in the walk-in cooler is very dim and must be increased. The kitchen fans require cleaning.

 3 0

Improper Construction/Maintenance/Sanitation of Establishment

Missing wall tiles in the kitchen must be replaced. Deteriorated wall surfaces, such as under the dishwash counter and above the bread cutting board, must be repaired. All wall finishes must be smooth, washable and non-absorbent. The counter where the bread is being cut is not an acceptable food preparation surface (even though a cutting board is being used). The bare plywood must be covered so that the surface is smooth, washable and non-absorbent. (Painting of the counter surface is not acceptable). Aluminum foil lining shelf surfaces must be removed. The walk-in cooler walls, floor, compressor, door and ceiling must be scrubbed clean to remove dirt, dust and mildew. The wooden shelves in the walk-in cooler must be replaced with shelves that are smooth, washable and non-absorbent. The cardboard under the slicer must be removed. The lid of the small chest freezer is dirty and is being held together with tape. This is not acceptable. A new lid must be installed.
