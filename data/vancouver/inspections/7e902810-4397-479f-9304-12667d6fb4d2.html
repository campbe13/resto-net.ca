Observations # of New Observations # of Resolved Observations

In Compliance - Food

Foodsafe or equivalent training requirement has been satisfied.

Mechanical dishwashing procedure is satisfactory (final rinse chlorine concentration is > 50 ppm).

No pests observed at time of inspection.

Washrooms are clean and well-supplied.

Thermometers are provided in every cooling unit containing perishable food.

 0 0

Lack of/Inadequate Food Safety Plan

Owner advises that he has completed his food safety plan. Please ensure your food safety plan is available for review when requested by the health department. Print out a copy and keep it at the restaurant. Section 23(2) of the BC Food Premises Regulation, failure to have written food handling procedures will result in ticketing of $230.

 1 0

Food/Water Adulterated or Received From Unapproved Sources

CORRECTIVE ACTIONS: Only purchase "grade A" eggs. Grade A eggs are crack free and clean. You must provide receipts/ invoice for all food products in your restaurant. Handout on Salmonella was delivered. Please be advised that under section 11 of the BC Food Premises Regulation, failure to obtain food from an approved source will result in ticketing of $230.

Observed 3 flats of eggs that are ungraded. Owner could not produce receipts to indicate that the eggs were purchased from an approved/inspected facility. Eggs were discarded during time of inspection.

 1 0

Food Not Protected From Contamination - Microbiological

Observed containers of raw meats stored above boxes of noodles and other cooked foods.

CORRECTIVE ACTIONS: Ensure raw meats are stored below cooked and ready-to-eat foods and vegetables. OR store raw meats and cooked foods/vegetables separately. Failure to comply with section 12 of the BC Food Premises Regulation, failure to protect food from contamination and handle food in sanitary manner -ticket $345.

 1 0

Food Not Protected - General

Buckets and containers of foods stored in walk-in cooler and sliding door cooler were kept uncovered and unprotected.

CORRECTIVE ACTIONS: Cover all foods (raw and cooked) with tight fitted lids or plastic wrap. This is to ensure that foods are protected from contamination. Section 12 of the BC Food Premises Regulation, failure to protect food from contamination will result in ticketing of $345.

 1 0

Improper Sanitary Facilities

No paper towels in kitchen hand sink and no paper towels at 2 compartment sink across from dishwasher.

CORRECTIVE ACTIONS: Ensure liquid soap and paper towels are available at all hand sinks. Under section 21(4) of the BC Food Premises Regulation, failure to supply and maintain adequate handwashing stations will result in ticketing of $230.

 1 0

Improper Construction/Maintenance/Sanitation of Establishment

Overall general sanitation has improved. However, more deep cleaning is required in the following areas: -cutting board across from dishwasher was worn out. Replace old cutting board with new ones. -2 compartment sink (across from dishwasher) require cleaning. -flooring and other hard-to-reach areas especially along cooking line require deep cleaning.

 1 0

Other - Misc

CORRECTIVE ACTIONS: Discard dirty towel and replace them with new ones. Ensure towels are submerged in 100ppm bleach solution at all times. Mix 1/2 teaspoon of bleach to 1 litre of water to make 100ppm bleach solution. Sanitize cutting boards, counters, food contact surfaces with bleach solution. Under section 17 of the BC Food Premises Regulation, fail to maintain premises, equipment and utensils in sanitary conditions will result in ticketing of $230.

Observed dirty towel used to wipe down counters, cutting boards and food contact surfaces. Dirty towels can hold bacteria and contaminate food contact surfaces. This was also noted in the last inspection dated in September.
