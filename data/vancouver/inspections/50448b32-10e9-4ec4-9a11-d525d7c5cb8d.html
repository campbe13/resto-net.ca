Lack of/Inadequate Food Safety Plan

Temperature recording stopped on October 25th. All but one cooler is being recorded as having temperatures of exactly 4 C, no matter what time of day. Daily temperature records are required to ensure that refrigerators are operating below the 4C standard to prevent the growth of pathogenic microorganisms. The manager was not aware of the existence of a Food Safety Plan. A written information package was provided to the manager to help him prepare a plan. Ensure an up to date Food Safety Plan is in place and approved by your inspector by December 2nd, 2011.

Monitor and record cooler temperatures daily. The Food Premises Regulation (B.C. Reg. 210/99) section 23 requires that: "every operator of a food service establishment must have written procedures to ensure that a health hazard does not occur in the operation of the establishment."

Food Not Protected From Contamination - Microbiological

At the time of the inspection, raw tuna was being processed for use in sushi. Large pieces of fish were observed to be held in one side of the small kitchen two compartment sink. This sink did not appear to be perfectly clean. Staff were directed to not put sushi fish directly into the sinks, but to hold the fish in clean containers. The raw tuna was being cut on cutting boards covered with paper rice bags that had been cut open. Staff were directed to only process the food on clean and sanitized cutting boards.

Ensure compliance with section 12 of the Food Premises Regulations (B.C. Reg. 210/99): Every operator of food premises must ensure that all food on the premises is (a) protected from contamination, and (b) stored, handled, prepared, displayed and dispensed in a sanitary manner. WARNING: Failure to comply with section 12 of the Food Premises Regulation may result in a violation ticket being issued ($345).

Inadequate Cooling and/or Refrigerated Storage of Potentially Hazardous Food(s)

An insert full of cooked chicken was observed "cooling" at room temperature in the cooking area. Staff were told to cool cooked food in more shallow layers in metal pans that are more shallow. Hot potentially hazardous foods, that will not be held hot, must be cooled quickly. Hot food must cool from 60 C to 21 C within a TWO hour period and from 20 C to 4 C within an additional FOUR hours. In order to meet this requirement action must be taken to ensure food is cooled quickly. Passively cooling food at room temperature for many hours is not acceptable. Recommendations on how to cool foods rapidly: >Divide large volumes of hot food into multiple smaller containers. Shallow pans are recommended. >Ice baths or ice wands may be used. >Stir cooling foods often. >Refrigerate (may not cool large quantities of food quickly).

Inadequate Cooling and/or Refrigerated Storage of Potentially Hazardous Food(s)

Prawns were observed to be held in a bucket of stagnant water in the dishwashing area two compartment sink. Staff were directed to thaw food submerged under constantly flowing cold water only. After the food has thawed, it must be immediately put into a cooler to hold at 4 C or below, if the food is not to be cooked immediately.

Improper Cleaning, Sanitizing of Equipment and Utensils

Operator must ensure compliance with Section 17 of the B.C. Food Premises Regulations: 17 (1) Every operator of food premises must ensure that the premises and the equipment and utensils used on the premises are maintained in a sanitary condition. (2) Without limiting subsection (1), every operator of food premises must ensure that the equipment, utensils and food contact surfaces used on the premises are washed and sanitized in a manner that removes contamination. WARNING: Failure to comply with Section 17 of the B.C. Food Premises Regulation may result in a violation ticket being issued ($230).

Bleach and water sanitizing solution was available at the time of the inspection, but in an un-labelled spray bottle. The sushi chef indicated that the sushi boards are only wiped with water, except at the end of the night when they are sanitized. >Food contact surfaces must be properly washed, rinsed, and sanitized after each use. >Cutting boards (or other food contact surfaces) must be cleaned and sanitized (using the bleach solution or another approved sanitizer) after use. >Clean and sanitize cutting boards or other equipment at least every four hours when they are under the same continuous use. Hold wiping cloths fully immersed in a bucket of sanitizing solution when not being actively used.

Inadequate Insect/Rodent Control

The operator must comply with section 26 of the Food Premises Regulations: Every operator of food premises must ensure that the premises are (a) free of pests, (b) free of conditions that lead to the harbouring or breeding of pests, and (c) protected against the entrance of pests. WARNING: Failure to comply with Section 26 of the Food Premises Regulations may result in additional violation tickets being issued ($230).

Mouse droppings were agin observed on the floor below the front sushi preparation counter. Mouse droppings were observed on the floor near the rear door behind stored boxes along the wall. Remove all stored items from the floor. All items must be stored off the ground on shelves. Place rodent traps along the rear wall near the rear door.
