Improper Cleaning, Sanitizing of Equipment and Utensils

>Ensure a sanitizer solution is available at all times in a bucket or spray bottle for sanitizing food contact surfaces. >Food contact surfaces must be properly washed, rinsed, and sanitized after each use. >Cutting boards (or other food contact surfaces) must be cleaned and sanitized (using the bleach solution or another approved sanitizer) after use. Clean and sanitize cutting boards or other equipment at least every four hours when they are under the same continuous use. >Improper two compartment sink method of ware washing., No sanitizing step is being done. After washing and rinsing dishes, they must then be sanitized by soaking items fully immerssed in an approved sanitizing solution for two minutes before the items are removed and air dried.

What appeared to be a Quats sanitizer was present in a spray bottle, but quaternary ammonium was not detected in this bottle. Staff say that dishes, containers and utensils are not routinely being sanitized after cleaning.

 2 0

Inadequate Insect/Rodent Control

Fill all holes, cracks, gaps to prevent pest entry into the kitchen. The operator must comply with section 26 of the Food Premises Regulations: 26 Every operator of food premises must ensure that the premises are (a) free of pests, (b) free of conditions that lead to the harbouring or breeding of pests, and (c) protected against the entrance of pests.

Rodent droppings observed. The rear door area requires pest proofing to prevent rodent access to the kitchen. The rear doors have a gap at the bottom where the doors meet. The roll down door seals well except on the left side were there is a two inch gap that could allow rodent access. Outside the rear doors there is a hole under the door jam on the left hand side. Some of the shelving units have bottom shelves that are two low to the ground to allow for easy cleaning beneath and pest control activities. All stored items should be stored off the floor on shelves at least 6" off the ground. Some rodent traps were observed to be inappropriately placed. Follow the pest control company's guidance on placement of traps to have the greatest chance of catching rodents. The washroom has gaps around the plumbing under the sink and there is a hole in the wall near the toilet.

 2 0

Food Handler Training

No food handlers on-site at time of inspection were FOODSAFE certified. A minimum of 1 trained food handler must be present on-site at all times food is being handled or prepared. No food was actively being prepared at the time of the inspection.

The Food Premises Regulation (B.C. Reg. 210/99) section 10(1) states: "Every operator of a food service establishment must hold a certificate, issued by a health official, for the successful completion of the food handler training program known as FOODSAFE or its equivalent. 10(2) Every operator of a food service establishment must ensure that, while the operator is absent from the food service establishment, at least one employee present in the establishment holds the certificate referred to in subsection (1)."
