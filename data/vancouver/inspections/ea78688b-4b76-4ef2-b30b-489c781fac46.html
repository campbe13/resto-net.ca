Inspection Information

Observations # of New Observations # of Resolved Observations

Lack of/Inadequate Food Safety Plan

The food safety plan posted on the walk-in cooler door does not meet the requirements outlined in the BC Food Premises Regulation. The plan must be expanded to include critical control points, critical limits., monitoring and corrective actions. Please refer to the information provided.

 1 0

Inadequate Cooling and/or Refrigerated Storage of Potentially Hazardous Food(s)

Potentially hazardous foods such as sandwiches and salads made with potentially hazardous ingredients were again being stored at room temperature. These foods were discarded by the inspector. Potentially hazardous foods must be stored or 4 degrees C or colder at all times. Immediate corrective action is required. Multiple warnings have been issued. Future non-compliance may result in the issuance of a violation ticket. Foods in the main preparation cooler were much too warm (pasta salad measured 8.8 degrees, Greek salad measured 8.6 degrees C, ham and pastrami both measured 11.8 degrees C). This is unacceptable. Potentially hazardous foods must be maintained at 4 degrees C or colder at all times. Small amounts of foods may be stored in ice if the foods are deeply submerged in the ice. Food temperatures must be monitored throughout the day. Multiple warnings have been issued. Future non-compliance may result in the issuance of a violation ticket.

 4 2

Inadequate Hot Holding of Potentially Hazardous Food(s)

Gravy in the hot-holding unit was again not sufficiently hot (47 degrees C). Foods must be at maintained at 60 degrees C or hotter at all times. Should foods in the hot-holding unit fall below 60 degrees C, then the foods must be re-heated to an internal temperature of at least 74 degrees C. This may only be done once. Should food temperatures fall below 60 degrees C a second time, the foods must be discarded. Potentially hazardous foods may not be stored at room temperature - not even during busy lunch rushes. Temperatures must be monitored throughout the day. Results are to be recorded. in a logbook. Multiple warnings have been issued. Future non-compliance may result in the issuance of a violation ticket.

 1 0

Lack of/Inadequate Sanitation Plan

The current plan must be expand to include daily, weekly and monthly cleaning tasks.

 1 0

Food Not Protected - General

Cooked bacon was being stored on egg cartons. This is unacceptable. Egg cartons potentially are covered with disease-causing bacteria. They are not acceptable for any type of food preparation or storage (other than whole raw eggs). Immediate corrective action is required.

Improper Construction/Maintenance/Sanitation of Establishment

All cardboard must be removed from the preparation table. All goods must be stored at least 6 inches (15 cm) up off of the floor. Dirty kitchen ceiling tiles must be cleaned. Dairy crates may not be used for food or equipment storage. Proper shelving or food preparation surfaces must be provided.
