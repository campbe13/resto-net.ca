Lack of/Inadequate Food Safety Plan

Food Not Protected From Contamination - Microbiological

Raw chicken skewers were observed to be held in an insert immediately beside an insert of cilantro (to be used as a garnish) in a kitchen preparation cooler. Corrected during the inspection. In the walk-in cooler, raw meat and other foods were for the most part well separated but some cooked food was observed to be held below raw meat.

>Ensure all raw meats are stored on lowest shelf in coolers (but off the floor), separated from other foods, in order to prevent potential direct contamination of food with dripping meat juices that can contain pathogenic microorganisms. Ensure compliance with section 12 of the Food Premises Regulations (B.C. Reg. 210/99): Every operator of food premises must ensure that all food on the premises is (a) protected from contamination, and (b) stored, handled, prepared, displayed and dispensed in a sanitary manner.

 1 1

Inadequate Cooling and/or Refrigerated Storage of Potentially Hazardous Food(s)

Ensure compliance with section 14 of the Food Premises Regulation (B.C. Reg. 210/99): 14 (1) Every operator of food premises that processes food must ensure that the food is processed in a manner that makes it safe to eat. (2) Every operator of food premises must ensure that potentially hazardous food is stored or displayed at a temperature of not more than 4�C or not less than 60�C.

At the time of the inspection, large containers of soup and meat were being cooled at room temperature, aided by an electric fan. The food was to be put into the walk-in cooler to cool further. The operator was directed to use active cooling techniques to ensure that food cools quickly and to verify cooling methods using a probe style thermometer. Hot food must cool from 60 C to 21 C within a TWO hour period and from 20 C to 4 C within an additional FOUR hours. In order to meet this requirement action must be taken to ensure food is cooled quickly. Passively cooling food at room temperature for many hours is not acceptable. Recommendations on how to cool foods rapidly: >Divide large volumes of hot food into multiple smaller containers. Shallow pans are recommended. >Ice baths or ice wands may be used. >Stir cooling foods often. >Refrigerate (may not cool large quantities of food quickly).

Improper Cleaning, Sanitizing of Equipment and Utensils

The chlorine bleach sanitizing solution in a spray bottle was measured to be too strong. Corrected during the inspection. >To prepare a 100 ppm bleach sanitizing solution add 1/2 teaspoon of bleach to every 1 liter of water used

Lack of/Inadequate Sanitation Plan

The employee washing dishes with the low temperature dishwasher was not aware how to monitor for adequate chlorine sanitizing solution concentration. Test strips were available. Ensure that staff monitor AND record the dishwasher sanitizer concentration daily.

 0 0

Improper Sanitary Facilities

Ensure compliance with Section 21 of the Food Premises Regulations: 21(3) Every operator of food premises must ensure that each employee washes his or her hands as often as necessary to prevent the contamination of food. 21(4) Every operator of food premises must supply and maintain handwashing stations adequate in number and location to ensure convenient access to all employees.

he time of the inspection, the kitchen hand washing sink was temporarily clogged with a small amount of food debris. Corrected during the inspection. A large cooking utensil was being held in this sink. Ensure that the hand sink is only used for hand washing. All other activities must be done in the other sinks. Ensure that there is always easy access to this sink.
