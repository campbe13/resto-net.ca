Food Not Protected From Contamination - Microbiological

At the time of the inspection, raw meat was not being held directly above vegetables or other foods. However, raw meat is being held on upper shelves but only directly above other containers of raw meat. Vegetables, including raw lettuce was being held beside the shelves holding the raw meat with very little horizontal separation. Staff were directed to arrange the food in the cooler so that all raw meat is held on the bottom shelves or to increase the horizontal separation between the raw meat and other foods.

>Ensure all raw meats are stored on lowest shelf in coolers (but off the floor), separated from other foods, in order to prevent potential direct contamination of food with dripping meat juices that can contain pathogenic microorganisms. Ensure compliance with section 12 of the Food Premises Regulations (B.C. Reg. 210/99): Every operator of food premises must ensure that all food on the premises is (a) protected from contamination, and (b) stored, handled, prepared, displayed and dispensed in a sanitary manner. WARNING: Failure to comply with section 12 of the Food Premises Regulation may result in a violation ticket being issued ($345).

Food Handler Illness/Hygiene/Habits

The staff member loading the mechanical dishwasher was observed to simply rinse his fingers with water at the two compartment sink after handling dirty ware and before handling clean dishes. He was reminded that hands must be properly and thoroughly washed using the available liquid hand washing soap and paper towel used for drying hands before touching cleand dishes.

 1 0

Inadequate Cooling and/or Refrigerated Storage of Potentially Hazardous Food(s)

Ensure that staff that are washing dishes and loading the mechanical dishwasher are not handling clean dishes with the same gloves that they used to handle dirty dishes. There should be designated gloves for cleaning dirty dishes and clean dishes. If bare hands are used to handle clean dishes, then hands must be properly and thoroughly washed before dishes are handled.

Large quantities of raw meat were not observed to be held at improper temperatures in the cooking area at the time of the inspection.

Improper Cleaning, Sanitizing of Equipment and Utensils

Staff say that coconut shells are no longer being re-used. After one use, the shells are discarded.

 1 1

Inadequate Insect/Rodent Control

>Fix the wall beneath the two compartment sink so that it is smooth and free of cracks and holes. The operator must comply with section 26 of the Food Premises Regulations: Every operator of food premises must ensure that the premises are (a) free of pests, (b) free of conditions that lead to the harbouring or breeding of pests, and (c) protected against the entrance of pests. WARNING: Failure to comply with Section 26 of the Food Premises Regulations may result in a violation ticket being issued ($230).

The wall beneath the two compartment sink has been repaired but there are still some holes around plumbing that must still be filled.. Items are still stored on the floor in the corner of the restaurant near the two compartment sink. Remove all stored items from the floor to allow easy cleaning of the floors all the way to the walls and to help with pest control activities.

 1 0

Improper Construction/Maintenance/Sanitation of Establishment

Operator must ensure compliance with Section 17 of the Food Premises Regulations (B.C. Reg.210/99): 17 (1) Every operator of food premises must ensure that the premises and the equipment and utensils used on the premises are maintained in a sanitary condition. (2) Without limiting subsection (1), every operator of food premises must ensure that the equipment, utensils and food contact surfaces used on the premises are washed and sanitized in a manner that removes contamination. WARNING: Failure to comply with Section 17 of the B.C. Food Premises Regulation may result in a violation ticket being issued ($230).

The walls and ceiling beside the cooking area have been cleaned to remove excessive grease build-up. However, many of the ceiling tiles are still brown with grease build-up. Replace all old brown ceiling tiles. The cleaning of the walls beside the cooking area has revealed or caused an un-smooth wall surface. Different layers of paint are exposed. The walls must be made to be smooth, impermeable to water and easy to clean.

 1 0

Improper Construction/Maintenance/Sanitation of Establishment

More frequent and thorough cleaning of the kitchen floor is required, especially between equipment in the middle of the kitchen and below cooking equipment.
