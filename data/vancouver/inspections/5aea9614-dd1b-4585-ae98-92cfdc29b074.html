Inadequate Cooling and/or Refrigerated Storage of Potentially Hazardous Food(s)

One half tray of cooked Gyoza was left on counter under serving window, sitting at 18 degree C, for greater than 2 hours. Instructed operator to discard the item.

Ensure food is cooled to room temperatore (from 60 to 20 degree C) within 2 hours, and store it in refrigerator to cool to 4 degree C within 4 hours. Never leave potentially hazardous food out at room temperature for over 2 hours. If longer than 2 hours, it must be discarded.

These water bottles are the same as other cookware and need to be properly sanitized after washing. Use the same 100ppm chlorine solution to sanitize bottles daily.

Squeezable sause bottles are reused as water bottles for generating steam on the grill. These plastic bottles are hand washed (cannot withstand high temperature in dishwasher) with warm water and soap, but not sanitized.

Improper Cleaning, Sanitizing of Equipment and Utensils

>A chlorine solution of 100ppm must be made at the start of each day everyday. Concentration and bleach strength will sustain overnight. >To make: 1 teaspoon of bleach for 1 liter of water. Immerse clean wiping cloth in the bleach water. Have this readily available for frequent use throughout the day. After each use you should replace the cloth back into the solution which will in effect re sanitize the cloth. Food preparation surfaces must be sanitized and allowed to dry in between different food groups and in any event on frequent occasions, meaning directly after lunch time and evening sessions and or every 2 hours. - This issue had been identified in previous inspections.

Chlorine sanitizor solution was at less than 10ppm. Operator advised solution was made on previous day.
