Inadequate Cooling and/or Refrigerated Storage of Potentially Hazardous Food(s)

Did not observe any potentially hazardous foods improperly cooled at the time of inspection. Ensure potentially hazardous foods that have been cooked and are intended to be kept under refrigerated storage prior to serving, are to be cooled from 60 oC (140oF) to 20 oC (68oF) or less within two hours and then from 20 oC (68oF) to 4 oC (40oF) or less within 4 hours. If the process takes any longer than this then discard foods. Equip the premise with a probe thermometer and ice wands to check temperature and cool down foods rapidly.

Improper Cleaning, Sanitizing of Equipment and Utensils

Resolved - Adequate amount of approved sanitizer at this time. Spray bottled containing diluted bleach (>100ppm Cl) were on hand for sanitizing surfaces and equipment throughout the day, when contamination of foods may occur, and to maintain the premise in sanitary conditions.

 1 1

Lack of/Inadequate Sanitation Plan

Develop and implement a written Sanitation Plan within 2 weeks time. Reference material will be provided. Provide a copy to the EHO for review.

OUTSTANDING - Operator must develop and implement a Sanitation Plan. As per Section 24 (1) of the BC Food Premise Regulation: every operator of a food service establishment must have written procedures to ensure the safe and sanitary operation of the establishment. (2) The written procedures required by subsection (1) must include (a) the cleaning and sanitizing requirements for the establishment and for all equipment and utensils used in the establishment, (b) the identification of cleaning and sanitizing agents used in the establishment, including their concentrations and their uses, and (c) the identification of all pesticides used in the establishment, including their uses and their storage requirements.

Inadequate Insect/Rodent Control

Resolved - rodent droppings that were observed in the back storage room on shelving and pallets where foods are being stored have been removed. The Owner has consulted with the landlord and entrance holes on the outside of the building are going to be filled in within the next few weeks. A pest control company has been hired for a period of 6 months.

The presence of rodents or insects must be treated immediately by inspecting and discarding any adulterated food. The inspection must be followed by: 1) cleaning and sanitizing surfaces contaminated by pests (i.e. by using 1000ppm diluted bleach wearing gloves) 2) removing dirt, soil or filth if present. 3) removing clutter or unnecessary items that may harbour pests or act as nests and breeding places 4) ensuring that all foods are stored in containers with tight-fitting lids. 5) protecting the food premises against the entrance of pests. 6) monitor for signs of pest activity on a daily basis - designate staff to check for droppings, use a flashlight if necessary, and carry out corrective actions listed above.
