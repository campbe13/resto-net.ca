Inadequate Cooling and/or Refrigerated Storage of Potentially Hazardous Food(s)

In the front cooking area, inserts of raw meat were being held inside the food preparation cooler so that the food is kept cold. Some inserts of cooked meat were still being held on top of the cooler. These inserts of meat were observed to be too full. The manager was directed to ensure that less food is held in the inserts to prevent potentially hazardous food items from being held at improper temperatures for extended periods of time.

Food stored above 4 C may allow the rapid growth of pathogenic organisms which increases the likelihood of causing a food borne illness in consumers. Operator must ensure compliance with Section 14(2) of B.C. Reg. 210/99: "Every operator of food premises must ensure that potentially hazardous food is stored or displayed at a temperature of not more than 4�C". WARNING: Failure to comply with Section 14(2) of the Food Premises Regulations may result in a violation ticket being issued ($345).

Lack of/Inadequate Sanitation Plan

Produce a sanitation plan (cleaning plan) indicating how surfaces in the kitchen are cleaned and how often, using which chemicals.

You must produce a sanitation, or "cleaning schedule", identifying which areas of the business must be cleaned, how they are cleaned, and how often. The schedule must detail which cleaning and sanitizing agents are being used in what concentrations, and how they are being used. The plan or schedule must also indicate who in the business is responsible for which tasks and when, so that owners or managers can check as to whether the work identified is being done. Section 24 of the BC Food Premises Regulation (B.C. Reg. 210/99) states: 24 (1) Every operator of a food service establishment must have written procedures to ensure the safe and sanitary operation of the establishment. (2) The written procedures required by subsection (1) must include: (a) the cleaning and sanitizing requirements for the establishment and for all equipment and utensils used in the establishment, (b) the identification of cleaning and sanitizing agents used in the establishment, including their concentrations and their uses.
