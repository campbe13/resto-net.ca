Food Establishment Inspection Detail

Ikura Japanese Restaurant

8646 Granville
Vancouver BC V6P 5A1
Primary Owner: Ikura Japanese Restaurant Ltd
Primary Operator: Hua Lin
Capacity: 48 Service Type: Restaurant - Full Service
Facility Web Site:

Inspection Details

Inspection Date: 14-Dec-2011
Inspector:
Follow-up Required: True
Reason for Inspection: Routine
Action Taken: Verbal Directive, Written Directive
Referrals:
General Comments: Routine inspection conducted with Senior EHO, B. Johnston. Results were discussed with owners Thuy and Andy. Sanitation and food handling methods were again found to be poor. Owners have been advised that they can no longer continue to operate their facility in this manner. Discussion took place with the owners regarding concerns, items requiring completion, future expectations, and future consequences for continued non-compliance. The following items must be addressed IMMEDIATELY: 1. Repair dishwasher so that it is achieving a final rinse chlorine concentration of >50 ppm 2. Clean and sanitize the ENTIRE facility thoroughly. This includes all corners, equipment, walk-in cooler and freezer, and hard-to-reach areas. 3. Develop a detailed sanitation plan outlining daily, weekly, monthly, and yearly tasks Operator has been given 2 weeks to have all items completed. Re-inspection will be conducted on December 28th. All corrective actions must be completed in full by then. FAILURE TO COMPLY WILL RESULT IN FURTHER ACTION SUCH AS TICKETING, CLOSURE, PERMIT SUSPENSION/RESTRICTION.
Closing Comments:

Inspection Information

Observations # of New Observations # of Resolved Observations

In Compliance - Food

Refrigeration temperatures are satisfactory (< 4 degrees C / 40 degrees F).

Freezer temperatures are satisfactory (< -18 degrees C / 0 degrees F).

Hot holding temperatures are satisfactory (> 60 degrees C / 140 degrees F).

 0 0

Lack of/Inadequate Food Safety Plan

1. Walk-in freezer was not equipped with a thermometer. 2. Observed lack of temperature monitoring/logging of coolers, freezers, and hot holding equipment.

1. Ensure all coolers and freezers are equipped with a thermometer to allow for temperature monitoring. 2. Ensure temperature of all coolers, freezers, and hot holding equipment is monitored and logged TWICE DAILY daily. A copy should be kept for at least 6 months. This is a record for the history of each unit and also could be used as evidence for a food borne illness investigation is required.

 1 0

Inadequate Cooling and/or Refrigerated Storage of Potentially Hazardous Food(s)

The following are acceptable thawing methods: >In a cooler < 4C >Under cold running water >In a microwave Corrected at time of inspection.

Observed tuna thawing at room temperature. Thawing at room temperature is not an acceptable thawing procedure as the temperature can not be monitored.

 1 0

Inadequate Hot Holding of Potentially Hazardous Food(s)

Sushi rice discarded at the time of inspection due to time/temperature abuse. Operator must time stamp the rice (e.g. using a timer or recording the time on a log) if it is to be stored at room temperature and then discarded after 2 hours. WARNING: Failure to comply may result in a violation ticket being issued under Section 14(2) of the BC Food Premises Regulation "Fail to store or display potentially hazardous food at a safe temperature." for $345

REPEAT INFRACTION: Observed sushi rice being stored at room temperature. Measured temperature of rice was 20C. Storing potentially hazardous foods at a temperature of greater than 4C and less than 60C encourages rapid growth of pathogenic microorganisms and must be discarded after 2 hours.

 1 0

Improper Cleaning, Sanitizing of Equipment and Utensils

Adjust/repair dishwasher IMMEDIATELY so that the final rinse is achieving a minimum of 50 ppm of chlorine at the dish level. In the meantime, single service dishes should be used as much as possible. All other dishes/utensils are to be washed manually using the following method: 1. Wash with warm water and detergent 2. Rinse with warm water 3. Sanitize using a bleach solution (1 oz bleach / 1 gallon water) From now on, use test strips to monitor sanitizer level, and take corrective actions if necessary. WARNING: Failure to comply may result in a violation ticket being issued under Section 17 of the BC Food Premises Regulation "Fail to maintain premises, equipment and utensils in sanitary conditions" for $230.

REPEAT INFRACTION: There was no sanitizer detected in the final rinse of the dishwasher.

 1 0

Improper Cleaning, Sanitizing of Equipment and Utensils

No sanitizing solution prepared/in use at the time of the inspection.

Sanitizer (e.g. 1/2 tsp bleach to 1L water) must be prepared daily and used on all food surfaces and equipment at the start of each day and throughout the day. Store solution in a spray bottle or in a bucket and ensure that it is available AT ALL TIMES.

 1 0

Lack of/Inadequate Sanitation Plan

Operator to develop a detailed sanitation plan outlining daily, weekly, monthly, and yearly tasks.

 1 0

Food Not Protected - General

Cover all foods prior to placing them into the cooler. Containers must not be stacked on top of uncovered food as the bottom of the container can contaminate the food underneath.

Observed uncovered containers of salmon skin stacked on top of each other in the walk-in cooler.

 2 0

Improper Sanitary Facilities

Handsoap was not available at the sushi bar handsink.

Ensure there is soap and paper towels available at all sinks at all times. Corrected at time of inspection.

 1 1

Inadequate Insect/Rodent Control

REPEAT INFRACTION: Observed rodent droppings in the front sushi bar area and in the back hallway. Presence of numerous mouse droppings on the premises indicates unacceptably low sanitation standards and ingress points for mice.

Remove the droppings from the indicated areas and examine the rest of the premises for any further evidence of pests. Clean and sanitize these areas. All food contact surfaces - counters, cutting boards, shelving, etc - must be cleaned and sanitized at the start of each day to prevent contamination of food and throughout the day. Check the premises for mouse droppings AT THE START OF EACH DAY and clean up as required. Keep all food, utensils and dishware in pest proof containers De-clutter and move all items (such as shelving) 6" away from the wall and off the floor to allow for easier cleaning and pest monitoring Seal all openings (>1/4") to prevent entry of pests in food premises. THERE SHOULD NOT BE ANY SIGNS OF PEST EVIDENCE IN FUTURE INSPECTIONS WARNING: Failure to comply may result in a violation ticket being issued under Section 26 of the BC Food Premises Regulation "Fail to ensure premises are free of pests" for $230.

 1 0

Improper Construction/Maintenance/Sanitation of Establishment

Clean and sanitize the entire kitchen thoroughly so it is spotlessly clean. This includes all corners and hard to reach areas. Operator must find a method to clean underneath the cookline as allowing this area to remain covered in grease and food is unacceptable. This must be done on a daily basis to prevent pest attraction.

REPEAT INFRACTION: There was a heavy accumulation of grease, moisture, and organic debris throughout the entire premises. Grease, moisture, and debris attract pests.

 1 0

Food Handler Training

Ensure copies of FoodSafe certificates for operator and staff are available onsite.
