Inadequate Cooling and/or Refrigerated Storage of Potentially Hazardous Food(s)

Potentially hazardous food must be kept hot at 60C or warmer at all times. If you are actually cooling the food you need to spread it out in a shallow layer, use ice/water baths, stir frequently to help it cool quickly. Food must cool from 60C down to 20C within 2 hours and then be placed in the cooler. Once in the cooler it has an additional 4 hours to drop down to 4C.

No cooked food found sitting on counters today- restaurant was not yet open.

Improper Cleaning, Sanitizing of Equipment and Utensils

Sanitizer in a spray bottle has been provided. Concentration is quite strong & it should be diluted. Use one teaspoon of bleach in one liter of water to make up the correct concentration. Use dishwasher test strips to test concentration. You want between 100-200ppm in the spray bottle.

Poor Sanitary Design, Construction, Installation of Equipment and Utensils

Tiles have been used over a variety of surfaces- these tiles are not a smooth surface, there are a number of grooves throughout and as such are not considered an acceptable surface. You are not being required to change them at this time however should problems arise or renovations be done you will be required to upgrade ot a more suitable surface.
