Lack of/Inadequate Food Safety Plan

A written food safety plan must be developed and submitted to Vancouver Coastal Health for approval by September 01, 2011.

 1 0

Food/Water Adulterated or Received From Unapproved Sources

A delivery of perch and clams was observed. Although a receipt was provided, it only indicated the dollar amount and not the name of the supplier. All food purchases require formal receipts which indicate the name ad address of the supplier.

 1 0

Inadequate Cooling and/or Refrigerated Storage of Potentially Hazardous Food(s)

A large container of minced fresh garlic was being stored at room temperature. This is a hazardous practice. Only small amounts of garlic may be stored at room temperature at a time to reduce the risk of botulism toxin formation. Only store enough to last 2 hours - remaining garlic must be refrigerated. Second Warning

 1 0

Inadequate Cooling and/or Refrigerated Storage of Potentially Hazardous Food(s)

The main preparation cooler was still much too warm, despite being repaired (food temperatures were till 10 degrees C). Also, rice noodles and coconut milk were being stored at room temperature. This is unacceptable. Potentially hazardous foods must be maintained at 4 degrees C or colder at all times. Immediate corrective action is required. Temperature monitoring must take place throughout the day. Results are to be recorded in a log book. Second warning. Always ensure that hot foods are cooled quickly. To hasten cooling, transfer large pots of curry, stock, etc. to shallow pots with a large surface area. Cooling can also be expedited by using an ice bath or ice wand and by stirring frequently. Second Warning. Rice noodles were soaking in the sink. Cold running water must flow over the noodles in the sink OR the rice noodles are to be re-hydrated in a bucket of water stored in the walk-in cooler.

 4 0

Inadequate Hot Holding of Potentially Hazardous Food(s)

Several pots containing curries were being stored at 45 degrees C. This is not acceptable. Immediate corrective action is required. Potentially hazardous foods must be maintained at 60 degrees C or hotter at all times. Temperatures must be monitored throughout the day. Should food temperatures fall below 60 degrees F, then the foods must be re-heated to an internal temperature of at least 74 degrees C. This may only be done once. Should the foods fall below 60 degrees C a second time, then foods must be discarded. Second Warning.

 2 0

Improper Cleaning, Sanitizing of Equipment and Utensils

The dishwasher was not consistently reaching a final rinse cycle temperature of 82 degrees C. Immediate corrective action is required. The dishwasher must be able to reliably reach 82 degrees C in the final rinse cycle. Chlorine test strips must be acquired to enable daily testing of the glasswasher's sanitizer concentration (50 ppm is required). Rice scoops were being stored in a container of water held at room temperature. This is not acceptable. Scoops must be cleaned and sanitized after each use or they must be stored in a manner that precludes bacterial growth - either at 4 degrees C or colder or at 60 degrees C or hotter. Immediate corrective action is required.

 3 0

Food Not Protected - General

Food trays and containers may not be stored in the back corridor. Second Warning. Paper towels must be used to absorb excess oil on fried rice noodles - not newspaper. Immediate corrective action is required.

 4 2

Inadequate Insect/Rodent Control

Please ensure that the back exit doors are rodent-proof. The back kitchen door must be kept closed to exclude the entrance of pests. Alternatively, a screen door must be installed. Monitor very closely for signs of rodent activity. Ensure that all rodent-control devices are not rotated or moved away from the walls. Doing so will render the devices ineffective.

 4 2

Improper Construction/Maintenance/Sanitation of Establishment

The ceiling tiles in front of the woks are very dirty and require cleaning or replacement. Second Warning.

Improper Construction/Maintenance/Sanitation of Establishment

The cardboard on the kitchen floor must be removed. The walk-in cooler floor and door require cleaning.
