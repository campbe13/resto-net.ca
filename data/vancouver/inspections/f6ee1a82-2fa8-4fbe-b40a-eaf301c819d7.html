Improper Cleaning, Sanitizing of Equipment and Utensils

> Place 1/2 tsp of bleach in every litre of water OR 1 ounce of bleach in every gallon of water > Prepare sanitizer solution at the start of every day. Sanitizer solution must be available in a spray bottle or in a rag bucket > All dirty/used dishcloths must be immersed in the sanitizer solution. Change the solution in the rag bucket every 2 to 3 hours. Section 17 (1) and (2) of the Food Premises Regulation states that "Every operator of food premises must ensure that the premises and the equipment and utensils used on the premises are maintained in a sanitary condition." and "Without limiting subsection (1), every operator of food premises must ensure that the equipment, utensils and food contact surfaces used on the premises are washed and sanitized in a manner that removes contamination." Failure to comply with the Food premises regulation may result in the issuance of one or more violation tickets, having your permit revoked, closure and/or court action.

FINAL WARNING, REPEAT INFRACTION: No sanitizer solution was available in a spray bottle or in a rag bucket at the time of inspection. The owner had a cleaning product that contained bleach in it, however the bottle was nearly empty and was not sufficient for sanitizing food contact surfaces. A container of bleach was available on-site at the time of inspection. Direction on how to prepare the sanitizer solution has been given on previous reports.

Improper Construction/Maintenance/Sanitation of Establishment

> Keep this area clear and empty in order to clean and monitor for pest activity. > Ensure that this area is properly pest proofed. Seal any new holes > Remove/discard all unused items from the facility. Section 18 of the Food Premises Regulation states that "An operator of food premises must not permit equipment or materials that are not required for the operation of the premises to be stored on the premises." Section 26 (b) of the Food Premises Regulation states that "Every operator of food premises must ensure that the premises are free of conditions that lead to the harbouring or breeding of pests." Failure to comply with the Food Premises Regulation may result in the issuance of one or more violation tickets, having your permit revoked, closure and/or court action.

Observed clutter and unused items stored in the upstairs area. The owner stated that he only uses the upstairs storage area for the storage of cups and coffee at the ledge of the storage area. Explained to the owner that all the clutter in the upstairs storage location creates a potential nesting area for pests. In order to monitor for rodent activity the upstairs area must be organized and clutter-free. Owner stated that he has fallen from the upstair area and does not want to injure himself again. Discussed situation with both the owner of Cafe Orca and with the hotel manager. The owner claims that the items, other than the coffee and cups, do not belong to him and was not placed in that space by him. The hotel manager explained that the upstair area was created by the previous tenant. Determining who is responsible for removing the items will be left up to the two parties. Suggested to hire a professional to remove all the unnecessary items from this area. Owner has agreed not to use this storage area.

Improper Construction/Maintenance/Sanitation of Establishment

WARNING, REPEAT INFRACTION: Observed floor/wall coving placed around the perimeter of the two-compartment sink. The strips were laid down over one another and has tiny pieces added in to fill the gaps. Floor/wall coving is not an acceptable material to be used on the counter around the sink. The material must be smooth, continuous, impervious to moisture and washable. Confirmed with the hotel management that the structural items located in the cafe are under the responsibility of the operator of Cafe Orca. The hotel managment is only responsible for the exterior walls. The owner of Cafe Orca has agreed to contact a contractor to replace the worn counter top around the two-compartment sink. Explained that the counter top around the coffee area must also be replaced in the near future, however the counter space around the two-compartment sink is of higher priority.

> Remove the floor/wall coving around the two-compartment sink > Refinish the counter top so that it is smooth, durable and impervious to moisture. Section 4(1) (a)(b) and (c) of the Food Premises Regulation states that "Every person who constructs or makes alterations to food premises must ensure that the food premises are of sound construction and in good repair, designed to ensure the safe and sanitary handling of food, constructed from materials that are suitable for their intended purpose, and durable, easily cleaned and free from any noxious or toxic substance" Failure to comply with the Food premises regulation may result in the issuance of one or more violation tickets, having your permit revoked, closure and/or court action.

Failure to have adequately trained operator, will result in the issuance of a Violation Ticket in the amount of $230.00.
