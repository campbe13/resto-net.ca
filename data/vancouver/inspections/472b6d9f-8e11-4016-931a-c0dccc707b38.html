Inadequate Insect/Rodent Control

The chronic mouse infestation appeared to have flared up as evidenced by the numerous droppings and mice caught in traps in the cafeteria. Hot spots included the teacher's lounge service counter (cupboards), dry storage room, service window cupboards & equipment and in the laundry area.

Mouse activity in a food premises is unacceptable and must be eliminated. >Contact your pest control technician to provide an Integrated Pest Management Plan to eliminate mice. >Check the storage room and discard any food items which show signs of chewing or contamination with mouse droppings or urine. -Remove rodent droppings with a paper towels and dispose off in the garbage. DISINFECT contaminated surfaces with BLEACH & WATER solution. -Prepare a fresh batch of sanitizing solution by mixing 1 ounce of chlorine bleach per gallon of water. >Provide rubber gloves for staff to wear during cleaning activities. After clean-up, food handlers must wash their hands, change clothing and put on a clean apron before preparing food.

 1 0

Improper Construction/Maintenance/Sanitation of Establishment

>Install a vacuum breaker on the faucet at the janitor sink or remove the hose attachment. >Replace or repair the damaged flooring in the walk-in cooler. >Install storage shelves to relief congestion around the work stations. >Patch & paint the kitchen interior.

No vacuum breaker/ backflow preventor on faucet at janitor sink by laundry area. Walk-in cooler flooring was damaged. Work stations appeared cluttered due to lack of storage shelves. Wall surfaces showed wear & tear.
