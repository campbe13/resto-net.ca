Food Not Protected From Contamination - Microbiological

A very large and heavy container of raw pork was observed to be held above the top shelf in the walk-in cooler. Vegetables and other foods were observed to be stored below the raw meat. Digital Photographs were taken as evidence. It is poor practice to hold anything below raw meat, as the food or containers may become contaminated by dripping blood/meat juices and lead to direct or cross contamination of other foods by pathogenic microbes.

>Ensure all raw meats are stored on lowest shelf in coolers (but off the floor), separated from other foods, in order to prevent potential direct contamination of food with dripping meat juices that can contain pathogenic microorganisms. Ensure compliance with section 12 of the Food Premises Regulations (B.C. Reg. 210/99): Every operator of food premises must ensure that all food on the premises is (a) protected from contamination, and (b) stored, handled, prepared, displayed and dispensed in a sanitary manner. WARNING: Failure to comply with section 12 of the Food Premises Regulation may result in a violation ticket being issued ($345).

Food Not Protected From Contamination - Microbiological

AGAIN, in the rear two compartment sink, multiple different foods were being thawed together in the same sinks. Raw chicken, raw fish and noodles were all being thawed in the same sink. This is poor practice. Care must be taken to avoid contaminating one food by another. Thaw raw chicken in one sink and seafood in a different sink. Clean and sanitize the sinks between different foods to prevent cross contamination.

Inadequate Cooling and/or Refrigerated Storage of Potentially Hazardous Food(s)

Improper thawing observed. A large strainer basket of raw prawns were observed to be held in a two compartment sink. The prawns were not immersed in cold running water as required and the surface temperature was measured to be 14 C.

Food must be thawed/defrosted in the refrigerator or fully immersed under cold running water. Operator must ensure compliance with Section 14(2) of B.C. Reg. 210/99: "Every operator of food premises must ensure that potentially hazardous food is stored or displayed at a temperature of not more than 4�C". WARNING: Failure to comply with Section 14(2) of the Food Premises Regulations may result in a violation ticket being issued ($345).
