In Compliance - Food

Handwashing facilites are well-supplied. Hot and cold running water, liquid soap and paper towel available. Refrigeration temperature(s) are satisfactory (< 4 degrees C / 40 degrees F). Hot-holding temperature(s) are satisfactory (> 60 degrees C / 140 degrees F). No signs of pests observed at time of inspection. Pest control program in place. Quat sanitizer (measured to be at 200 ppm) is satisfactory. Food handling practices appear to be satisfactory.

 0 0

Lack of/Inadequate Food Safety Plan

Temperature logs not kept up to date.

Temperature readings of all coolers, freezers and hot-holding units (if any) are to be taken and recorded daily to ensure that equipment are working properly and that potentially hazardous food is stored at the proper temperatures.

 1 0

Inadequate Cooling and/or Refrigerated Storage of Potentially Hazardous Food(s)

All potentially hazardous foods are to be stored in a working cooler maintained at 4 degrees C or less to slow down the growth of pathogenic bacteria.

Some sauces and shredded cheese in the counter-top cooler measured to be at 8 degrees C. Advised the operator to cover the foods with tight-fitting lids/covers or to store a smaller amount prior and after the lunch rush.

 1 0

Inadequate Cooling and/or Refrigerated Storage of Potentially Hazardous Food(s)

Rice found to be cooling at room temperature (measured to be at 31 degrees C with an infra-red thermometer) - operator stated it had been cooling for nearly two hours. Recommended to place the containers of cooked rice into a larger container filled with ice and water to help cool the rice quicker.

All potentially hazardous foods are to be cooled from 60 degrees C to 21 degrees C within 2 hours and from 20 degrees C to 4 degrees in the following 4 hours. Foods are not to be left at room temperature to cool down for prolonged periods of time to prevent the growth of pathogenic bacteria and/or their toxins.

 1 0

Inadequate Hot Holding of Potentially Hazardous Food(s)

Gyros measured to be at 45 degrees C with an infra-red thermometer. Upon discussion, the staff member had told me that they slow cook the gyros to ensure the outside does not burn. Explained to him that foods need to be cooked quickly and that the food is to be hot-held at 60 degrees C or above.

All potentially hazardous foods are to be maintained at 60 degrees C or less to prevent the growth of pathogenic bacteria.

 1 0

Food Not Protected - General

Uncovered cut potatoes in aluminum trays found to be double-stacked on top of one another. Aluminum tray found to be used as a cover for raw meat skewers in the walk-in cooler - corrected at time of inspection.

Foods are to be covered with tight-fitting lids or covers and are not to be double-stacked to prevent contamination of the food.

 2 1

Improper Construction/Maintenance/Sanitation of Establishment

All food contact surfaces are to be smooth and non-absorbent. Cardboard is not to be used as it cannot be easily cleaned and sanitized and will absorb moisture and dirt, which can lead to contamination of food and/or equipment. Clean equipment is to be stored properly in a way that prevents contamination. Unused or unnecessary equipment is to be removed from the premises in order to facilitate the ease of cleaning and sanitizing (1 oz. bleach in 1 gallon water) of surfaces.

Cardboard found to be used to line some of the shelving units in the walk-in cooler - operator stated it was to prevent the grooves from forming on the pita bread (which happens if stored directly on the rack). Trays for serving food to customers on found to be stored directly on the floor underneath the counter-top cooler unit - operator stated they were not in use.

 2 0

Improper Construction/Maintenance/Sanitation of Establishment

Hard-to-reach areas underneath the preparation table with the one sink found to be accumulated with food debris.

The noted area as well as other hard-to-reach areas are to be cleaned and sanitized (1 oz. bleach in 1 gallon water) on a regular basis to remove the food debris and prevent the attraction of pests.

 1 0

Food Handler Training

No staff member at the time had FoodSafe or equivalent training - staff member stated that the other staff that had FoodSafe had just left and that another staff with FoodSafe was starting their shift soon.

At least one staff member with FoodSafe or equivalent training is to be on shift at all times.
