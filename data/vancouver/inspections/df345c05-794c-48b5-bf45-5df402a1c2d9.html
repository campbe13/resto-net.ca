In Compliance - Food

High temperature dishwasher was tested and recorded at 73.1 C, at the final rinse cycle. Temperature logs were provided for all coolers, within the facility.

Food Safety Plan has been implemented and is in use.

Sanitation Plan has been implemented and is in use.

Foodsafe or equivalent training requirement has been satisfied.

Refrigeration temperatures are satisfactory (< 4 degrees C / 40 degrees F).

Freezer temperatures are satisfactory (< -18 degrees C / 0 degrees F).

Inadequate Cooling and/or Refrigerated Storage of Potentially Hazardous Food(s)

Foods, submerged in the ice wells, were found on top of the ice. This is a repeated offense. Potentially hazardous food products must be stored at 4 C and below. Fully submerge the inserts, until the lip of the insert.

Potentially hazardous food items must be stored at 4 C and below. Foods must be stored fully within the ice wells, if not then this product must be placed into a refrigerated cooler. In this type of situation, the food items stored at the top of the insert fall into the Danger Zone and could potentially lead to the growth of microorganisms. Depending on the flow and volume of the particular food product, it cannot be stored within 4 C to 40 C.

Inadequate Cooling and/or Refrigerated Storage of Potentially Hazardous Food(s)

Garlic oil product was stored at room temperature. This product, stored in this fashion, will proliferate the growth of botulism spores. Garlic oil was removed and disposed, at the time of the inspection. It is recommended to add garlic to the oil, upon the time of the order, instead of mixing this product in advance and storing it at inappropriate temperatures.

 1 0

Inadequate Hot Holding of Potentially Hazardous Food(s)

Two beef roasts were stored on the cutting board. One was stored under the heating lamp and the other roast was not, it was resting at room temperature. This product cannot be stored at this temperature. When hot holding food items, the food product must be stored at 60 C and above. Provide a subsequent heat lamp or store the food product in the hot holding case.

Improper Cleaning, Sanitizing of Equipment and Utensils

Observed a leak with the hot water line, leading to the tea area. Ensure this is fixed and not wrapped with a dishcloth. CO2 cannisters must be chained and attached to a hard surface.

 2 0

Improper Cleaning, Sanitizing of Equipment and Utensils

The quaternary ammonium sanitizer, located in the three compartment sink, was tested and recorded at 500-600 ppm. Reduce the concentration to a minimum of 200 ppm by dilution with water. Litmus strips were supplied and must be used to determine the concentration of the sanitizer.

 1 0

Inadequate Insect/Rodent Control

Rodent droppings were found in the hard to reach areas, under the shelving units and in the far right corners. Ensure these areas are cleaned and droppings are removed immediately.

Remove all droppings. Clean and sanitize areas with a bleach-water sanitizing solution (1 part of bleach to 10 parts of water: 500 ppm). Clean kitchen and keep clean. Ensure all foods are stored in pest proof containers with tight fitting lids. Abell Pest Control conducts services on a routinely basis, continue providing glue boards in the highly impacted areas. Rodent proof the building by sealing holes with steel wool and spray foam, and keeping the back door closed at all times. Provide additional mouse traps, including snap traps, glue/ sticky boards, and tin cat (metal box) style traps.

 1 0

Improper Construction/Maintenance/Sanitation of Establishment

Do not use aluminum foil on the grill line, this perceives lack of cleaning. The area must be routinely cleaned and sanitized, when aluminum foil is placed here, it is difficult to sanitize the respective areas. Remove the rodent droppings in the preparation areas. Clean and sanitize all hard to reach areas, especially under the shelving units.
