Food Not Protected From Contamination - Other Sources

> Remove the plastic wrap prior to opening the bar for service.

Observed plastic wrap around the nozzles of the beer drafts. The chef explained that the plastic is used to prevent the presence of fruit flies in the bar overnight. Instructed the chef to remove the plastic wrap prior to the start of every day. After cleaning the nozzles every night new clean plastic wrap may be placed on.

 1 0

Inadequate Cooling and/or Refrigerated Storage of Potentially Hazardous Food(s)

Measured the sauce in the ice well to be at 8 C to 12 C at the time of inspection. More ice is added during the day, however all potentially hazardous foods must be maintained at 4 C or colder. Instructed the staff to move the inserts to the walk-in cooler until the dinner rush. Inserts will be discarded after the dinner rush and must not be kept for the next day. Explained to the chefs that all inserts must be kept in the walk-in cooler until each service rush. Inserts for the lunch rush will be taken out and placed in the ice well for a maximum of two hours. This must also be done for the dinner rush. Any left over food in the inserts must be discarded at the end of each rush. Recommended that this unit be converted to a mechanical prep cooler unit.

> Food must be kept at 4 C or colder or at 60 C and higher. Otherwise all potentially hazardous foods must be discarded/used within 2 hours.

Improper Sanitary Facilities

> Restore the hot water supply to this washroom. > Clean with soap and warm water, then sanitize using 500ppm bleach solution. (2 to 3 tsp of bleach per litre of water) Section 21(4) of the Food Premises Regulation states that "Every operator of food premises must supply and maintain handwashing stations adequate in number and location to ensure convenient access to all employees." As defined in the interpretation section, "A handwashing station" means a hand basin provided with (a) hot and cold running water, (b) soap in a dispenser, and (c) a method of hand drying that uses single service products Failure to comply with the Food Premises Regulation [B.C. Reg. 210/99] may result in the issuance of one or more violation tickets, having your permit revoked, closure and/or court action.

Measured no hot water in the staff washroom. This washroom requires routine cleaning. This washroom was closed off at the time of inspection. Technician suspected that there is a problem with one of the pumps and will be working to resolve this issue as soon as possible.
