Lack of/Inadequate Food Safety Plan

A written food safety plan must be developed. Second Warning. Please forward your completed food safety plan to Vancouver Coastal Health for approval before December 10, 2011.

 1 0

Inadequate Cooling and/or Refrigerated Storage of Potentially Hazardous Food(s)

Tofu, pre-cooked chicken, pre-cooked vegetables, deep fried chicken wings, beef teriyaki, rice noodles in water, and other potentially hazardous foods were being stored at room temperature. The beef teriyaki and the pre-cooked chicken measured 16 degrees C. According to staff, washed rice was being stored at room temperature for at least an hour. Foods were defrosting on the floor at room temperature. Deliveries of meat were sitting on the floor at room temperature. These are unacceptable food handling practices. Immediate corrective action is required. Potentially hazardous foods must be maintained at 4 degrees C or colder at all times. Potentially hazardous foods may not be stored at room temperature over busy lunch and dinner periods - no exceptions. Only remove foods from the cooler when needed. Temperature monitoring must take place throughout the day and the results must be recorded in a logbook. Third Warning. Future non-compliance may result in the issuance of a violation ticket.

 2 0

Lack of/Inadequate Sanitation Plan

A written sanitation plan must be developed. Second Warning. Please forward your completed sanitation plan to Vancouver Coastal Health for approval before December 10, 2011.

 1 0

Food Not Protected - General

Mildew was visible in the icebox and on the beverage lines that intersect the icebox. Also, filth was noted on the inside of the beverage dispenser holder. Ice intended for human consumption must be protected from contamination at all times. The icebox, the beverage lines and the dispenser holder must be cleaned and sanitized on a daily basis . Immediate corrective action is required.

 1 0

Food Not Protected - General

Food containers were again being stored inside other food containers. This is an unacceptable food handling practice. Food containers may only being stacked on top of each other when tight-fitting lids are in place. Immediate corrective action is required. Second Warning. The use of water containers for the room temperature storage of rice scoops, ice cream scoops, knives, etc. must be discontinued immediately. Third Warning. Knives, scoops and other utensils must be cleaned and sanitized after use or must be stored in a manner that precludes bacterial growth (either at 4 degrees C or colder or 60 degrees C or hotter). Future non-compliance may result in the issuance of a violation ticket. Bowls were being used to dispense dry goods. This is not acceptable. Scoops with handles must be used to dispense dry goods. Immediate corrective action is required.

 3 0

Improper Sanitary Facilities

Liquid soap must be provided at the hand wash sink located immediately outside of the kitchen.

 1 0

Improper Construction/Maintenance/Sanitation of Establishment

The stand in the sushi bar requires thorough cleaning. The plastic wrap must be removed from the front of the deep fryers. Second Warning The white shipping wrap must be removed from the table in the tempura area. The plastic wrap dispenser in the sushi bar must be cleaned. The fumehood canopy requires professional cleaning. The dirty wooden board under the bag of flour must be removed. All goods must be stored in food-grade containers at least 6 inches up off of the flour.

 7 1

Food Handler Training

At the time of inspection, no kitchen food handlers had completed Foodsafe (only one of the servers had taken Foodsafe). A lack of staff trained in safe food handling principles was readily apparent when inspecting the kitchen since a multitude of poor food handling practices was observed. WHENEVER THE RESTAURANT IS IN OPERATION, AT LEAST ONE FOOD HANDLER, IN ADDITION TO THE MANAGER, MUST BE PRESENT AT ALL TIMES WHO HAS TAKEN FOODSAFE OR AN EQUIVALENT FOOD HANDLER TRAINING PROGRAM. Immediate corrective action is required.

Written documentation must be on-site with respect to the percentage of trans fat in your menu items. Second Warning.
