Inspection Information

Observations # of New Observations # of Resolved Observations

Lack of/Inadequate Food Safety Plan

Staff were unsure whether a food safety plan was in place. Please ensure that a copy of your approved food safety plan is on the premises at all times and that staff are familiar with the contents and location of the plan. Second warning.

 1 0

Inadequate Hot Holding of Potentially Hazardous Food(s)

The spicy pork measured 47 degrees and egg rolls measured 53 degrees C. This is not acceptable. Staff were requested to take immediate corrective action. Potentially hazardous foods must be maintained at 60 degrees C or hotter at all times. Food temperatures must be monitored and recorded throughout the day, especially during or immediately after busy lunch and dinner periods.

 1 0

Food Not Protected - General

The large white cutting board on the main preparation table was filthy on both sides. This cutting surface must be replaced. Also, the wall surface above the cutting board requires thorough cleaning. Mildew was present on the inside of the ice machine. This is unacceptable. The ice must be removed and the inside surfaces of the ice machine must be properly cleaning and sanitized. Ice must be protected form contamination at all times. Food containers were being stored directly on top of eachother. This is not an acceptable food handling practice. Food storage containers may only be stored on top of one another when tight -fitting lids are in place.

 7 4

Inadequate Insect/Rodent Control

Mouse droppings were noted throughout the food storage lockers. Dried mouse urine and droppings were noted on the floor, and in the room where sugar and rice is stored. In addition, many of the rodent control devices were sprung or moved away from the walls. This is totally unacceptable. All rodent excrement must be removed. Rodent abatement strategies must be increased. The dry storage rooms must be made rodent-proof. Items being stored in the dry storage rooms must be re-arranged so that all areas are readily accessible at all times. All shelves must be cleaned. Goods must be stored at least 6 inches (15 cm) up off of the floor in rodent-proof containers. All rodent control devices must be re-set and placed along the walls or along equipment for maximum effectiveness. Immediate corrective action is required.

 1 0

Improper Construction/Maintenance/Sanitation of Establishment

Thorough cleaning is required under and adjacent to the deep fryer cabinets. The compressors in both walk-in coolers must be cleaned to remove mold and dust. The chest freezers in the dry storage room must be cleaned to remove ice build-up and spilled food items. The shipping film must be removed from the walk-in cooler door and stainless steel table in the dry storage room. All items in the storage locker that are not essential to the day-to-day operation of the restaurant must be removed. The moisture and grease in the vicinity of the dry goods containers must be removed. The upstairs walk-in cooler floor was filthy. Thorough cleaning of the floor must take place. Food trays had sauce spattered on them. This is not acceptable. Food trays must be protected from contamination at all times. The trays must be cleaned. Thorough cleaning is required under the woks to remove heavy accumulations of grease and filth.
