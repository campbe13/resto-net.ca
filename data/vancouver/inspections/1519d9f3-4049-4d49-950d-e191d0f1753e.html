Lack of/Inadequate Food Safety Plan

Temperature readings of all coolers, freezers and hot-holding units (if any) are to be taken and recorded daily to ensure that equipment are working properly and that potentially hazardous food is stored at the proper temperatures.

RECURRING: Temperature records not available at time of inspection.

 1 0

Inadequate Cooling and/or Refrigerated Storage of Potentially Hazardous Food(s)

All potentially hazardous foods are to be stored in a working cooler maintained at 4 degrees C or less to slow down the growth of pathogenic bacteria. The counter top cooler should be closed when not in use to prevent any major temperature fluctuations.

RESOLVED: Various foods in the counter top cooler were properly covered with a food-grade cover to prevent temperature flucuation. Items were measured to be below 4 degrees C.

 1 1

Improper Cleaning, Sanitizing of Equipment and Utensils

RESOLVED: Sanitizing solution was available for use at time of inspection.

Sanitizing solution (1 oz. bleach in 1 gallon water) is to be prepared fresh daily and located in a convenient area for use whenever necessary.

 1 1

Food Not Protected - General

All food is to be stored off the ground (at least 6 inches) to facilitate ease of cleaning and sanitizing of surfaces. Food contained in perforated bags will have an increased risk of contamination. Therefore, ensure that the onions are stored off the floor to prevent potential contamination. Ensure that cooler fans are cleaned and sanitized to prevent the potential contamination of food.

RESOLVED: Buckets of food stored directly on the floor of the walk-in cooler. NEW: Perforated bag of onions were stored on the floor at the time of the inspection. Prep and Walk-in cooler fans were observed to be accumulated in filth, debris and mould.

 3 1

Food Not Protected - General

RECURRING: Lots of single-use containers and lids found stored in cardboard boxes across from the dishwasher.

The single-use containers are to be removed and all containers used to store food are to be of food-grade material with a tight-fitting lid. All food contact surfaces are to be smooth and non-absorbent. Cardboard boxes are not be used as they cannot be easily cleaned or sanitized and will absorb moisture and dirt, leading to contamination.

 1 0

Food Not Protected - General

Proper food-grade scoops with handles are to be used for the different foods in the dry storage bins to prevent contamination of the food. One scoop is to be used for each different bin.

RESOLVED: Proper scoops were used in the dry food storage bins.

 1 1

Food Not Protected - General

RECURRING: Light cover found to be missing above 2 compartment sink.

All light fixtures are to be properly covered to prevent contamination of food with glass in case of breakage.

 1 0

Improper Sanitary Facilities

RESOLVED: Men's washroom equipped with papertowels.

All hand-washing stations are to be supplied with hot and cold running water, liquid soap and paper towel at all times to allow for effective and proper hand-washing to prevent contamination.

 1 1

Inadequate Insect/Rodent Control

NEW: Droppings noted underneath the sink below the dishwasher. Garage door open at the time of the inspection.

The premises, especially hard-to-reach areas, are to be cleaned and sanitized frequently. Rodent droppings are to be cleaned and the area sanitized using a bleach solution (1 oz. bleach in 1 gallon of water) as part of monitoring process. Ensure that the garage door is closed as much as possible during operational hours to prevent the entrance of pests. Pests are known to contaminate food and spread disease.

 2 0

Improper Construction/Maintenance/Sanitation of Establishment

RESOLVED: Hard-to-reach areas (edges and underneath racks) in the walk-in cooler were free from food debris. Egg carton trays were not found lining the floor underneath one of the racks in the walk-in cooler. NEW: Two gaps were observed below the compartment sink by the dishwasher.

The egg carton trays are to be removed as they cannot be easily cleaned or sanitized and will absorb moisture and dirt. Hard-to-reach areas in the walk-in freezer and cooler are to be cleaned and sanitized (1 oz. bleach in 1 gallon water) on a regular basis to prevent accumulation of food debris. Ensure that all cracks and crevices are properly sealed to prevent the entrance and harbourage of pests. It was noted that mouse droppings were observed by the gaps.

 3 2

Other - Operating Without a Valid Permit

The new decal is to be placed on the health permit and the permit is to be posted in a conspicuous location.

RESOLVED: Health permit posted in a conspicuous location.
