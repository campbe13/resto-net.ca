Food Not Protected From Contamination - Other Sources

1. Hands must be washed and dried thoroughly before wearing gloves, when changing to a new pair of gloves, and after removing the gloves 2. A new pair of gloves must be worn: - When changing tasks (e.g. when moving to a new work station) - After covering mouth during sneezing or coughing, blowing nose, or touching hair - As frequently as possible. A pair of gloves should not be worn for longer than 4 hours. Prolonged use of a single pair of gloves can result in excess perspiration on hands, which provides ideal conditions for bacterial growth on the skin. Gloves are also more likely to leak or tear if worn for extended periods. 3. Disposable gloves should only be used once. 4. Durable re-usable gloves must be washed and sanitized between tasks and stored carefully after removal to prevent contamination. 5. Gloves should always be worn by employees who have cuts, sores, or rashes on hand

Observed lunch service administered by students. Overall it went well - good practices were observed: using tongs to serve pizza and salad, assigned specific duties, washing hands before and after glove use, sneezing in the sleeve, etc... Proper glove use was not consistent - some students were multitatsking using the same gloves i.e. swtiching between serving food and handling other equipment without washing hands and changing gloves in between.
