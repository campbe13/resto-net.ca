Lack of/Inadequate Food Safety Plan

Information on how to write a food safety plan issued to the operator. It is a requirement under the Health Acts Food Premises Regulation to have written food safety procedures. Please read the information supplied and start procedures for some of the potentially hazardous items on the menu. Will discuss during next inspection. According to the Food Premises Regulation, Section 23 (2) Every operator of a food service establishment must have written procedures to ensure that a health hazard does not occur in the operation of the establishment. (3) The written procedures required by subsection (2) must include (a) identification of all critical control points, (b) critical limits for those critical control points, (c) the procedures to be followed to ensure adherence to the critical limits, and (d) the actions to be taken in the event that the critical limits are not adhered to.

No food safety plan/procedures available. - not done A reasonable amount of time has pasted and information was provided to complete the food safety plan. A further month is given to have plan completed by 20 July 2011. Further delay may result in a fine under the food Premises Regulations Section 23 (2) Failure to have written food handling procedures. $ 230.00.
