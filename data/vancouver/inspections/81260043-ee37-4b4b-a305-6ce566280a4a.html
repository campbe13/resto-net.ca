Inadequate Cooling and/or Refrigerated Storage of Potentially Hazardous Food(s)

Re-occurring observation/Outstanding - potentially hazardous food temperature in excess of 4 degrees C. Some potentially hazardous foods (PHFs) in inserts in the prep cooler are now being stored in metal inserts however, not all potentially hazardous foods. PHFs in the right side of the cooler in metal inserts = 4.1 degrees C. Middle plastic inserts = 8.9 C, left plastic inserts = 6.2 C. Discussed with staff about refraining from storing PHFs in inserts for more than 2hours between 4-60 degrees C, otherwise discard. Recommended using metal inserts for all potentially hazardous foods.

Again, all potentially hazardous foods and foods labeled "keep refrigerated" must be stored at 4 degrees C or below at all times. - have a technician service the cooler to provide more air flow on to the bottom/top of the inserts. - store food items in bottom of prep cooler and take out only what is needed for small periods of time in small portions. - foods must be stored below the level of the insert in order to be kept cool. - keep lid for prep cooler closed as much as possible to keep foods cool. - replace plastic inserts with metal inserts. - keep foods in inserts for a maximum of 2 hours if the cooler can not maintain foods at <4 degrees C.

Inadequate Insect/Rodent Control

A few rodent droppings were observed on the floor in the dry storage room where building materials have not been lifted. One dropping on shelving in the back room where the alcohol is being stored. Produce stored off the ground on shelving. Holes in the dry storage room have been filled. Sanitation plan has been up-dated to include pest control

Further corrective actions must take place immediately: - again, seal all gaps and holes (move shelving away from walls and fill holes in wall, flooring, and ceiling). Do a complete survey of the building (inside and out) and fill all holes in storage room (in office above?) and delivery door. - again, clean-up droppings and sanitize surfaces contaminated with droppings. Recommend to refrain from vacuuming. Spray the urine and droppings with a disinfectant or a mixture of bleach and water (concentration of bleach solution is 1 part bleach to 10 parts water) and let soak 5 minutes. Use a paper towel to pick up the urine and droppings, and dispose of the waste in the garbage. After the rodent droppings and urine have been removed, disinfect items that might have been contaminated by rodents or their urine and droppings. - store all open foods in pest proof containers (i.e. plastic totes). - clean and sanitize any food debris and grease daily. DAILY REQUIREMENTS: - see last follow-up inspection report
