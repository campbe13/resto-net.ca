Lack of/Inadequate Food Safety Plan

The employee present was not aware if the temperatures of hot held food and cooler temperatures are being monitored and recorded as required by a Food Safety Plan. Monitor and record temperatures twice daily. If a Food Safety Plan is not in place in the restaurant, a plan must be prepared and approved by your inspector. Your inspector can provide you with information on how to prepare a Food Safety Plan.

The business is required by law to have an up to date Food Safety Plan that details how food is safely handled from receipt to sale or consumption. This plan must include the identification of critical control points (CCPs) and critical control limits (CCLs) which must be monitored daily and documented. The plan must describe the process followed to ensure adherence to the critical limits, and the actions to be taken in the event that the critical limits are not adhered to. The Food Premises Regulation (B.C. Reg. 210/99) section 23 requires that: "every operator of a food service establishment must have written procedures to ensure that a health hazard does not occur in the operation of the establishment."

Improper Cleaning, Sanitizing of Equipment and Utensils

The employee present was not aware of the presence of any sanitizing solution and when and where he would used sanitizing solution. An unlabelled spray bottle containing bleach and water was located. It was measured to contain an excessively strong (>1000 ppm) bleach and water sanitizing solution. >Ensure a sanitizer solution is available at all times in a bucket or spray bottle for sanitizing food contact surfaces. >To prepare a 100 ppm bleach sanitizing solution add 1/2 teaspoon of bleach to every 1 liter of water used. >Food contact surfaces must be properly washed, rinsed, and sanitized after each use. >Cutting boards (or other food contact surfaces) must be cleaned and sanitized (using the bleach solution or another approved sanitizer) after use. >Clean and sanitize cutting boards or other equipment at least every four hours when they are under the same continuous use. Hold wiping cloths fully immersed in a bucket of sanitizing solution when not being actively used.

Operator must ensure compliance with Section 17 of the B.C. Food Premises Regulations: 17 (1) Every operator of food premises must ensure that the premises and the equipment and utensils used on the premises are maintained in a sanitary condition. (2) Without limiting subsection (1), every operator of food premises must ensure that the equipment, utensils and food contact surfaces used on the premises are washed and sanitized in a manner that removes contamination. WARNING: Failure to comply with Section 17 of the B.C. Food Premises Regulation may result in a violation ticket being issued ($230).

Inadequate Insect/Rodent Control

Signs of a rodent infestation were observed during the inspection. Rodent droppings were observed on brown paper bags below the front counter and on the floor below the rice container on the milk crate, and near the washroom. >Remove all rodent droppings. Clean and sanitize areas contaminated by rodents with a bleach and water solution (1 tsp. bleach per 1 liter water). >Thoroughly clean kitchen and keep it clean. >Ensure all open packages of food are stored in pest proof containers. >Obtain services of a professional pest control company to help you eliminate the infestation, and follow the pest control company's recommendations. >Maintain rodent traps in appropriate locations. >Rodent-proof the restaurant (eg seal all cracks, gaps, and holes etc). >Do not use milk crates as shelves. Remove the milk crate to allow for the floor to be more easily cleaned. Everything should be stored off the ground at least 6" to facilitate cleaning of the floors and facilitate pest control.

The operator must comply with section 26 of the Food Premises Regulations: Every operator of food premises must ensure that the premises are (a) free of pests, (b) free of conditions that lead to the harbouring or breeding of pests, and (c) protected against the entrance of pests. WARNING: Failure to comply with Section 26 of the Food Premises Regulations may result in a violation ticket being issued ($230).

Food Handler Training

The Food Premises Regulation (B.C. Reg. 210/99) section 10(1) states: "Every operator of a food service establishment must hold a certificate, issued by a health official, for the successful completion of the food handler training program known as FOODSAFE or its equivalent. 10(2) Every operator of a food service establishment must ensure that, while the operator is absent from the food service establishment, at least one employee present in the establishment holds the certificate referred to in subsection (1)." WARNING: Failure to comply with Section 10 of the Food Premises Regulation may result in a violation ticket being issued ($230).

The only employee present at the time of the inspection does not have Foodsafe certification. A minimum of 1 trained food handler must be present on-site at all times food is being handled or prepared. >Immediately register other food handlers so that this requirement can be met. See website at: http://www.foodsafe.ca
