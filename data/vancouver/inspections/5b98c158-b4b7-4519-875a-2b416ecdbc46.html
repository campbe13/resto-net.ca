Inadequate Cooling and/or Refrigerated Storage of Potentially Hazardous Food(s)

Ensure compliance with section 14 of the Food Premises Regulation (B.C. Reg. 210/99): 14 (1) Every operator of food premises that processes food must ensure that the food is processed in a manner that makes it safe to eat. (2) Every operator of food premises must ensure that potentially hazardous food is stored or displayed at a temperature of not more than 4�C or not less than 60�C. WARNING: Subsequent violations of section 14 of the Food Premises Regulations (B.C. Reg. 210/99) may result in a violation ticket being issued ($345).

Again a large amounts of cooked chicken was observed in a deep inserts. Large volumes of food take much more time to cool that the same food cooled in smaller volumes or in thinner layers in shallow metal pans. Hot potentially hazardous foods that will not be held hot must be cooled quickly. Hot food must cool from 60 C to 21 C within a two hour period and from 20 C to 4 C within an additional four hours. In order to meet this requirement action must be taken to ensure food is cooled quickly. Passively cooling food at room temperature for many hours is not acceptable. Recommendations on how to cool foods rapidly: >Divide large volumes of hot food into multiple smaller containers. Shallow pans are recommended. >Ice baths or ice wands may be used. >Stir cooling foods often.
