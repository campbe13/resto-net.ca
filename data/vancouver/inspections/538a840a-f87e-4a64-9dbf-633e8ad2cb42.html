Inadequate Cooling and/or Refrigerated Storage of Potentially Hazardous Food(s)

Have both coolers serviced in order to maintain potentially hazardous foods at 4 degrees C at all times. In addition, - monitor coolers throughout the day and take necessary corrective actions if needed. - remove items from display cooler that impede air flow. - evenly distribute hot foods amongst all coolers so that foods can cool properly.

1. Under-counter prep cooler measured 10 degrees C at the time of inspection. Plenty of hot foods were placed in the cooler within 1.5 hours of the inspection raising the temperature of the cooler. 2. Poor air circulation noted for front display cooler. Some areas of the cooler were maintaining potentially hazardous foods between 7-11 degrees C despite both thermometers near the fan registering 3-4 degrees C. Operator claims that the freezer is capable of freezing food items first thing in the morning.
