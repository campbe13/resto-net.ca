Food/Water Adulterated or Received From Unapproved Sources

CRITICAL HAZARD: -Found same small jars of oil in the kitchen of this premises as was observed in the shed at the owner/operators' residential property. Also, this product was only labelled in Chinese, to which the male owner/operator admitted that they were imported directly from China.

The owner/operators of this premises are directed to immediately remove all unapproved food and food equipment from this premises, including product that came from their house or directly from China without the approval of the Canadian Food Inspection Agency (CFIA). Furthermore, immediately cease and desist all unapproved import of food and food equipment for use in this premises, as this practice is unsafe and is strictly prohibited. Product found onsite without approved labels (English and French language) will be immediately seized or destroyed and additional enforcement action may be taken.

 1 0

Improper Cleaning, Sanitizing of Equipment and Utensils

Immediately discard all food in these live seafood tanks and thoroughly clean and sanitize these tanks to a high standard before returning seafood to them. Live seafood tanks require continuous maintenance to ensure the safety of the food stored in them. Failure to do so endangers the safety of the seafood consumed by customers.

CRITICAL HAZARD: -The inside of the two live seafood tanks are filthy.

 1 0

Lack of/Inadequate Sanitation Plan

The owner/operators of this premises are directed to submit a Sanitation Plan, including a proposed cleaning and sanitation schedule and procedures for this premises to the undersigned Environmental Health Officer at Fax # 604-233-3175 by February 5, 2012. This Sanitation Plan will be used to increase the frequency and effectiveness of cleaning this premises to prevent the poor conditions observed today.

 1 0

Food Not Protected - General

Immediately remove all bowls from dry food bins and replace with foodgrade scoops with handles only AND place covers on all foods in storage in coolers, freezers, and dry storage. These tasks must be done by January 5, 2012.

-Bowls stored in several dry ingredient bins used to scoop these foods. -Many foods stored uncovered in the walk-in cooler.

 2 0

Poor Sanitary Design, Construction, Installation of Equipment and Utensils

Chopping block to east side of kitchen is extremely worn.

Discard this chopping block immediately.

 1 0

Improper Sanitary Facilities

-Two hoses observed hanging either in thawing food or otherwise below the waterline of two sinks in the kitchen, presenting a possible cross connection hazard. -Papertowel dispenser empty at the only kitchen handsink. -Papertowels located a distance away from the handsink in the waiters' station.

-Immediately remove these hoses OR cut them to not hang below the waterline (top rim) of these sinks. -Immediately refill the papertowel dispenser in the kitchen OR replace the papertowel dispenser with one that can be easily refilled. -Immediately install a fixed papertowel dispenser directed behind the waiters' station handsink for easy access and frequent use.

 4 0

Inadequate Insect/Rodent Control

-Large quantity of mouse droppings observed on shelving in the dry storage room onsite. No evidence of current rodent activity. -Gap observed at the base of the rear screen door to this premises, with no door sweep/seal in-place.

The owner/operator of this premises is directed to immediately install a door sweep that effectively seals the rear door of this premises to the floor and closes all gaps that may allow the entry of pests, such as mice. They were also directed to thoroughly clean and sanitize the dry storage room, among other areas, and remove all rodent droppings from the premises.

 2 0

Improper Construction/Maintenance/Sanitation of Establishment

This premises must be completely cleaned and sanitized to a high standard by January 5, 2012 (2 weeks). Explained and pointed out specific areas to be included in this cleaning, such as flooring underneath shelving, the walk-in cooler fan shrouds, and the ceiling tiles in the kitchen. -Missing or cracked floor tiles and coving tiles must be replaced by February 5, 2012 (6 weeks). -Missing range hood filters must be replaced by January 5, 2012 (2 weeks). -Reach-in cooler handle must be replaced by January 5, 2012 (2 weeks). -Wall behind chopping block must be resurfaced to a smooth and impervious finish by February 5, 2012 (6 weeks). -Open drain pipe must be capped/sealed by January 5, 2012 (2 weeks).

-Observed unclean surfaces throughout the kitchen and back rooms of this premises. When questioned, a food handler stated that areas of the kitchen such as behind work tables have not been cleaned in about two months. -Ceiling tiles are missing, extremely filthy/stained, or peeling. -Flooring and coving tiles are cracked or missing. -Range hood filters missing in places. -Wall behind the chopping block is extremely worn/unsmooth and requires resurfacing. -Reach-in cooler door handle is broken and missing. -Uncapped/open drain pipe in the old staff washroom, now used for storage.
