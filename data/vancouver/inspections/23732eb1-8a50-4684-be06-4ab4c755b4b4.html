Lack of/Inadequate Food Safety Plan

A Food Safety Plan is required. Daily monitoring and recording of cooler temperatures is required. (Two times daily). The business is required by law to have an up to date Food Safety Plan that details how food is safely handled from receipt to sale or consumption. This plan must include the identification of critical control points (CCPs) and critical control limits (CCLs) which must be monitored daily and documented. The plan must describe the process followed to ensure adherence to the critical limits, and the actions to be taken in the event that the critical limits are not adhered to.

The Food Premises Regulation (B.C. Reg. 210/99) section 23 requires that: "every operator of a food service establishment must have written procedures to ensure that a health hazard does not occur in the operation of the establishment." "critical control point" means a location in a food service establishment or a step in the establishment's procedures where failure to comply with the Act or this regulation may result in a health hazard; "critical limit" means standards that must be met to ensure that a health hazard does not occur at a critical control point.

Food Not Protected From Contamination - Microbiological

In the walk-in cooler a container of raw chicken was observed to be held above raw vegetables.

>Ensure all raw meats are stored on lowest shelf in coolers (but off the floor), separated from other foods, in order to prevent potential direct contamination of food with dripping meat juices that can contain pathogenic microorganisms. Ensure compliance with section 12 of the Food Premises Regulations (B.C. Reg. 210/99): Every operator of food premises must ensure that all food on the premises is (a) protected from contamination, and (b) stored, handled, prepared, displayed and dispensed in a sanitary manner. WARNING: Failure to comply with section 12 of the Food Premises Regulation may result in a violation ticket being issued ($345).

Improper Cleaning, Sanitizing of Equipment and Utensils

Chlorine test strips were not available to test for adequate chlorine concentration in the glass washer and dishwasher. Ensure test strips are always available and that the sanitizer concentration is tested and recorded daily.

Inadequate Insect/Rodent Control

>Remove all rodent droppings. Clean and sanitize areas contaminated by rodents with a bleach and water solution (1 tsp. bleach per 1 liter water). >Place rodent traps in appropriate locations. >Rodent-proof the restaurant (eg seal all cracks, gaps, and holes etc). >The services of a professional pest control company may be required if this problem persists. The operator must comply with section 26 of the Food Premises Regulations: Every operator of food premises must ensure that the premises are (a) free of pests, (b) free of conditions that lead to the harbouring or breeding of pests, and (c) protected against the entrance of pests.

Signs of mouse activity were observed in the restaurant. A few mouse droppings were observed in a downstairs storage room and in the boiler room.

 1 0

Improper Construction/Maintenance/Sanitation of Establishment

The shelving units in the downstairs kitchen hallway have bottom shelves that are too low to allow for easy cleaning of the floor beneath. Raise the bottom shelves to provide a 6" gap below.

Food Handler Training

The operator must become Foodsafe Certified.

The Food Premises Regulation (B.C. Reg. 210/99) section 10(1) states: "Every operator of a food service establishment must hold a certificate, issued by a health official, for the successful completion of the food handler training program known as FOODSAFE or its equivalent. 10(2) Every operator of a food service establishment must ensure that, while the operator is absent from the food service establishment, at least one employee present in the establishment holds the certificate referred to in subsection (1)."
