Improper Cleaning, Sanitizing of Equipment and Utensils

At the time of the follow-up inspection, the dishwasher final rinse was reaching an improper temperature for sanitizing again. The EHO let it run 3 times - measured 62 degrees C, 58 degrees C, and 62 degrees C for each run. For mechanical dishwashing the final rinse water temperature must be > 71 degrees C / 160 degrees F for 10 seconds, measured at the dish; or >82 degrees C/179 degrees F, measured at the manifold in order to adequately sanitize.

RESOLVED: The final rinse temperature of the dishwasher was adequate for sanitizing (temperature measured 73.8 C measured at the plate.

Food Not Protected - General

At the time of the follow-up inspection, the staff indicated they did not have temperature sheets on hand for recording monitored temperatures. In addition, they were not aware if a probe thermometer was present at the premise or not (from the last inspection report - see below). 1. Inaccurate thermometers in one of the cooling unit. The temperature of the prep cooler in the front measured 4 degrees C however, the thermometer read 0 degrees C. 2. No probe thermometer present for monitoring the temperature of hot foods.

RESOLVED: 1. Accurate thermometers in each cooler. Daily temperature records available for viewing. 2.Premise is equipped with a probe thermometer to monitor the temperature of hot foods.
