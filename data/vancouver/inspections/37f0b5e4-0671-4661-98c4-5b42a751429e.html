Improper Cleaning, Sanitizing of Equipment and Utensils

Discard disposable cups after each use or switch to food-grade containers (e.g., food-grade plastic, glass, metal) for the storage of food ingredients. Disposable cups are not suitable for reuse as they deteriorate over time and cannot be adequately washed with the facility equipment. Ensure that a sanitizer solution containing at least 100 ppm Chlorine is available at all times for sanitizing food contact surfaces. Prepare the solution from the original bleach jug rather than premixing the solution for storage. Chlorine levels deteriorate over time especially when premixed and exposed to light. DISCUSSED WITH OPERATOR ALLOWABLE REUSABLE CONTAINERS. IMMEDIATELY CEASE AND DISCARD THE ONE-TIME USE CONTAINERS USED TO PORTION PRODUCT.

Disposable plastic cups were washed and reused for storage of ingredients for blended drinks. Sanitizer bucket contained a bleach solution with 0 ppm Chlorine. A premixed bleach solution was stored in a labelled milk jug which also contained 0 ppm Chlorine.

Improper Sanitary Facilities

Handwashing sink was out of paper towels.

Ensure warm water, liquid soap and paper towels are available at the handwashing sink at all times for staff to properly wash their hands. DISPOSABLE PAPERTOWELS AVAILABLE.

Food Handler Training

At all times, the owner or a worker (in absence of owner) having FOODSAFE training or equivalent must be present in the establishment. BC Food Premises Regulation section 10 states: "Foodsafe training 10 (1) Every operator of a food service establishment must hold a certificate, issued by a health official, for the successful completion of the food handler training program known as FOODSAFE or its equivalent. (2) Every operator of a food service establishment must ensure that, while the operator is absent from the food service establishment, at least one employee present in the establishment holds the certificate referred to in subsection (1). " DISCUSSED WITH OPERATOR THE ABOVE REQUIREMENTS.

Owner was not present at time of inspection and foodhandler on premises did not have FOODSAFE training or equivalent.

Other - Misc

Two refrigerators were missing thermometers and one refrigerator had a broken thermometer. Temperature logs are not kept and not used. OPERATOR OBTAINED FOR COOLERS.

Equip all refrigeration units with functional and accurate thermometers. Monitor refrigeration units and record temperatures into a temperature log at least twice a day (once in the morning hours and once in the afternoon hours). Monitoring temperatures and keeping logs are important in identifying problems with refrigerators and adjusting temperatures when necessary. Template temperature log provided.
