Food Not Protected From Contamination - Microbiological

A jug of iced tea was uncovered and found at the bottom shelf of a kitchen refrigerator. No raw meat was found above the jug. Chef stated that the jug of iced tea was for his personal use. (Put at top shelf at time of inspection).

Do not store foods/drinks that do not require further cooking on bottom shelves. Drippage from raw meat onto the food/drink can cause cross-contamination and foodborne illness. Keep raw meats at bottom shelves and ready-to-eat foods and vegetables at top shelves. Cover containers of food and drink to prevent things from falling in.

 1 1

Food Handler Illness/Hygiene/Habits

Wash hands thoroughly after handling wiping rags, raw foods or any other contaminants. Use the handwashing sink for handwashing purposes, and the food prep sink for food prep purposes.

Food handler not washing hands thoroughly before resuming work after using a wiping rag. When handwashing was observed, the food prep sink rather than the handwashing sink was used.

 1 0

Inadequate Cooling and/or Refrigerated Storage of Potentially Hazardous Food(s)

Refrigerate eggs to 4 degrees C or less. Implement a procedure where eggs brought in are immediately put into the refrigerator and off the floor. This will minimize the risk that bacteria will grow to levels that can cause foodborne illness.

A box of stacked trays of eggs was on the kitchen floor measured at 14.4 degrees C. Chef stated that the eggs came in this morning prior to the inspection. (Eggs put into refrigerator at time of inspection).

 1 1

Other - Misc

Store food related items in designated food areas (e.g., rack for trays, proper hangers for tongs). Install a working and accurate in the cooler containing the milk. Keep temperature logs on premises so that it can be used everyday.

Food related items were stored in non food related places (e.g., deep frying bin stored on boiler, tongs hung on electrical wire, trays stored between counters on the floor). Thermometer is missing in the front cooler containing the milk. Temperature log was not kept on premises at time of inspection. Operator stated that sous chef had taken it home but that monitoring is done twice a day and recorded into the log.
