Flying Wedge Pizza Co

1059 Denman
Vancouver BC V6G 2M7
Primary Owner: Vincent Enterprises Ltd
Primary Operator: Christopher D'Souza
Capacity: 16 Service Type: Food Establishment - Limited Service
Facility Web Site:

Inspection Details

Inspection Date: 12-Oct-2011
Inspector:
Follow-up Required: False
Reason for Inspection: Re-inspection
Action Taken: Information Exchanged, Verbal Directive
Referrals:
General Comments: Mathew, one of the joint operators present at time of inspection. Inspection primarily to check on temperature/time control of pizzas on display and staff training in food safety. Both of which were found still to be in contravention of the Food Premises Regulation.
Closing Comments: A further follow up will be carried out in due course at which time it is expected that all outstanding work will have been completed.

Inspection Information

Observations # of New Observations # of Resolved Observations

Inadequate Cooling and/or Refrigerated Storage of Potentially Hazardous Food(s)

Main Prep Fridge THE FOLLOWING CONTRAVENTION HAS NOW BEEN RESOLVED - This was measured at between +12C and +17C in different parts of the fridge. Other refrigeration should be used until this equipment has been repaired to ensure a maximum temperature of +4C at all times.

 1 1

Improper Cleaning, Sanitizing of Equipment and Utensils

Kitchen Utensils THE FOLLOWING CONTRAVENTION HAS NOT BEEN RESOLVED - At the time of the inspection pizza preparation cooking utensils were left in water at room temperature on the preparation fridge counter. Utensils should be either cleaned after each and every use, stored at +4C or colder, stored in approved sanitizer or stored at a temperature of +60C or hotter to control growth of bacteria. You should refer to your company food safety policy for instruction. Grease Trap THE FOLLOWING CONTRAVENTION HAS NOT BEEN RESOLVED - There is no evidence of when the grease trap was last cleaned, this should be provided at time of follow-up inspection to ensure waste drainage of all sinks is free flowing. Wiping Cloths THE FOLLOWING CONTRAVENTION HAS NOT BEEN RESOLVED - At the time of the inspection wiping cloths were noted stored at room temperature. To prevent risk from growth of bacteria these should be clean, dedicated in use and stored in approved sanitizer when not being used.

 3 0

Improper Cleaning, Sanitizing of Equipment and Utensils

Degreaser/Sanitizer THE FOLLOWING CONTRAVENTION HAS NOT BEEN RESOLVED - Neither degreaser or sanitizer were observed in use or readily available at time of inspection. Both degreaser for cleaning and sanitizer for disinfecting should be provided in clearly labeled spray bottles for example and be readily available for use. This should be in accordance with you company food safety policy.

 1 0

Improper Construction/Maintenance/Sanitation of Establishment

Cleaning THE FOLLOWING CONTRAVENTION HAS NOT BEEN RESOLVED - Cleaning in all of the more difficult to reach areas should now and routinely be thoroughly cleaned to prevent risk from pests. In particular behind/beneath the chest freezer. Freezer THE FOLLOWING CONTRAVENTION HAS NOT BEEN RESOLVED - The freezer is in need of cleaning. This should now and routinely be defrosted and cleaned. Dishwasher THE FOLLOWING CONTRAVENTION HAS NOW BEEN RESOLVED -The dishwasher was leaking. This should now be repaired.

 3 1

Food Handler Training

Food Safety Training THE FOLLOW CONTRAVENTION HAS NOT BEEN RESOLVED - At the time of the inspection a lone worker was present who has not completed a recognised food safety training course. At all times the facility is in use, a member of staff should be present who has completed Foodsafe Level I training or equivalent. - a lone member of staff was working who had received training to Foodsafe Level I standard.

 1 0

Other - Misc

Thermometers THE FOLLOWING CONTRAVENTION HAS NOW BEEN RESOLVED - No thermometer was provided in the main preparation fridge. Accurate thermometers should be provided in each and every refrigerator and temperatures monitored at least once daily.

 1 1

Other - Misc

Time/Temperature Record Keeping THE FOLLOWING CONTRAVENTION HAS NOT BEEN RESOLVED - At the time of the inspection pizzas on display at room temperature were not being temperature monitored. In line with your food safety policy, pizzas on display at room temperature should be monitored to ensure they do not remain for sale in excess of 2 hours. After 2 hours they should be discarded. Accurate temperature monitoring must now be carried out and available for inspection. -pizzas were again observed without documentation to show length of time on display or timers being set. Hand Wash Station THE FOLLOWING CONTRAVENTION HAS NOT BEEN RESOLVED - At the time of the inspection it appeared that the hand wash station in the rear kitchen was being used to clean food off of utensils. This is a dedicated hand wash. It should only be used for hand washing and should now be repaired to ensure efficient waste drainage.
