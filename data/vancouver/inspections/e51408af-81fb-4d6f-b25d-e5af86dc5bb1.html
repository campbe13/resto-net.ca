Inadequate Cooling and/or Refrigerated Storage of Potentially Hazardous Food(s)

Ensure that potentially hazardous foods are to be cooled in a manner that prevents pathogen growth. Recommended methods include the use of an ice bath or a cooling wand to ensure that cooked foods are cooled from cooking temperatures (74 degress C) or hot-holding temperatures (60 degrees C) to 20 degrees C within 2 hours and then directed into a refrigerated storage unit so that the food item can be cooled from 20 degrees C to 4 degrees C within 4 hours. Failure to do so will increase the risk pathogen growth and foodborne illness.

Two plastic containers of a cream-based soup was seen cooling at room temperature. Lone kitchen staff immediately corrected this at the time of the inspection.

Inadequate Insect/Rodent Control

Ensure all pest monitoring devices are properly serviced by the contracted pest control technician to facilitate efficient pest monitoring. If all traps are changed in a regular manner, attention can be directed in specific areas of known pest activity which will make it much more managable to control the pests.

The two rodent traps in the cooler was accumulated in grease and filth. One trap needs to be replaced as it was observed to contain rodent droppings and the upper see-through plate was removed. No other signs of pest activity was evident in the cooler. All the other traps in the facility had been serviced and replaced recently as they were all in good working condition. Manager noted that the contracted pest control company comes once a month and their last visit was 3 days ago.

Improper Construction/Maintenance/Sanitation of Establishment

Ensure that the hole is properly sealed off to prevent the entrance of pests. Pests are known to be serious contaminators and can severely affect the sanitation quality of any establishment depending on the level of infestation. Ensure that nearby areas along the walls are also monitored for potential entry/exit points for pests to properly keep them out. Ensure that the flooring underneath the ventilation canopy is properly cleaned and sanitized to prevent the accumulation of food debris and grease to prevent the risk of pest harbourage. Deep-fry areas accumulated in food debris and grease are known to be an excellent source of warmth, moisture and food that could increase the risk of pest infestations. Ensure that all surfaces that come into contact with mouse droppings are to be cleaned and sanitized with 100 ppm of bleach solution (1 oz. bleach per 1 gallon of water) to facilitate proper pest monitoring.

A hole (diameter of aproximately 4 cm) in the wall directly above the PVC pipe below the stainless steel table by the dishwasher was found. This was brought to the attention of the Manager and the Chef. The Chef noted that the Pest Control Service Technicians were aware of the hole but has yet to pest-proof it. Mouse droppings were observed below the sink and the dishwasher. Accumulation of grease, filth and french fries were found below the deep fryer. Stainless steel walls behind the deep fryer were observed to be very clean though.
