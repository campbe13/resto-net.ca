Food Not Protected From Contamination - Microbiological

Proper thawing includes thawing in the refrigerator (food must be pulled a day or two ahead of time) or thawing under cold RUNNING (not standing) water. Cold running water was turned on over the food item during the inspection This is the third time that improper cooling has been observed.

OBSERVED: Frozen meat was observed thawing in standing water, meat was still frozen at this point. This is not a proper thawing method - it encourages growth of pathogens.

 1 1

Inadequate Cooling and/or Refrigerated Storage of Potentially Hazardous Food(s)

OBSERVED: Grated cheese and creamy salad dressings stored in the prep display cooler at 12C

Potentially hazardous food items must be stored at 4C or less or -18C or less when not in use to reduce bacterial growth. Ensure that these items are stored in the prep cooler when not in use to maintain temperature. The items were re-located to under the prep cooler during the inspection.

 1 1

Improper Cleaning, Sanitizing of Equipment and Utensils

Wiping cloths are to be stored in a 200ppm concentration of sanitizer to prevent bacterial growth when not in use. Ensure that sanitizer is made with 1tsp of bleach to 1 litre of water and that cloths are stored in it from now on.

OBSERVED: wiping cloths stored on the counter at room temperature.

Food Not Protected - General

OBSERVED: garbage bags used to cover food items such as the french breads

Garbage bags are not made to store food and thus can potentially leak harmful chemicals into the food. Re-store the bread and other food items thst are stored in garbage bags in in food grade bags or containers.
