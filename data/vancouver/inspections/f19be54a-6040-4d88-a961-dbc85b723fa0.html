Owner/Operator does not have a written Food Safety Plan. As per Section 23 (2) of the BC Food Premise Regulation "Every operator of a food service establishment must have written procedures to ensure that a health hazard does not occur in the operation of the establishment. The written procedures required by subsection (2) must include (a) identification of all critical control points, (b) critical limits for those critical control points, (c) the procedures to be followed to ensure adherence to the critical limits, and (d) the actions to be taken in the event that the critical limits are not adhered to".

Food Not Protected From Contamination - Microbiological

Resolved - did not observe scoops for dispensing steamed rice stored in warm water a this time This creates a situation for bacterial growth to occur and thus contamination of foods if the utensil is used again. Wash and sanitize the utensil between each use and store the utensil in a dry sanitized container. Otherwise, store utensils in ice water to keep the temperature of the solution below 4 degrees C.

 1 1

Inadequate Cooling and/or Refrigerated Storage of Potentially Hazardous Food(s)

Resolved - did not observe cooked chicken in the prep inserts not properly cooled down after cooking. All potentially hazardous foods that have been cooked must be properly cooked (i.e. to 74 degrees C) and then properly cooled and refrigerated (i.e. from 60 C (140F) to 20 C (68F) or less within two hours and then from 20 C (68F) to 4 C (40F) or less within 4 hours) prior to re-heating to 74 degrees C (165 degrees F) or above. Half cooking potentially hazardous food is a high risk practice. These requirements must be reflected in your Food Safety Plan.

 1 1

Lack of/Inadequate Sanitation Plan

Owner/Operator does not have a written Sanitation Plan. As per Section 24 (1) of the BC Food Premise Regulation "Every operator of a food service establishment must have written procedures to ensure the safe and sanitary operation of the establishment. (2) The written procedures required by subsection (1) must include (a) the cleaning and sanitizing requirements for the establishment and for all equipment and utensils used in the establishment, (b) the identification of cleaning and sanitizing agents used in the establishment, including their concentrations and their uses, and (c) the identification of all pesticides used in the establishment, including their uses and their storage requirements".

Devise and implement a Sanitation Plan. A Sanitation Plan and Food Safety Plan have been due since this April. Provide a copy of a written Sanitation Plan to the Environmental Health Officer via email, fax, or mail within 2 weeks time.

Improper Sanitary Facilities

Resolved - hand sink in the bar area was not obstructed at the time of inspection and was equipped with single-use paper towels. All handwashing stations must be fully equipped with hot/cold running water, liquid soap and papertowels. Hand sinks must be accessible for use.

 1 1

Improper Sanitary Facilities

Resolved - hand washing sink in kitchen area has been equipped with un-diluted liquid soap. Providing the proper equipment ensures foodhandlers are able to properly wash their hands to prevent contamination of foods. A proper hand wash consists of: vigorously rubbing together the surfaces of the lathered hands and exposed arms with soap and warm water for at least 20 seconds followed by a thorough rinsing with warm clean water. Dry hands with single-use paper towels and use the towel to turn off the water faucets to avoid re-contaminating hands.

Inadequate Insect/Rodent Control

Again, rodent droppings observed amongst food containers and cutting boards on a shelf under the dishwashing area and on the floor behind the up-right cooler. Did not observe rodent droppings on shelving next to chopping knives, on the floor in the staff washroom, and on the floor in the women's washroom. This is now the fourth consecutive inspection where rodent droppings have been observed at this premise. Staff claim that they have a pest control contract. Note: pest control companies are not responsible for the DAILY cleaning, sanitizing, inspection for droppings, and removal of droppings when observed. This is the Owner's responsibility.

Owner/Operator must: 1. Inspect and discard any adulterated food (including foods in open containers). 2. Clean and sanitize surfaces contaminated by pests (i.e. by using 1000ppm diluted bleach). Remove all items off of shelving and sanitize shelving and food containers. 3. Remove all food debris and grease from inside and outside the premises on a daily basis 4. Protecting the food premises against the entrance of pests - seal all gaps and holes (move shelving away from walls and fill holes in wall, flooring, and ceiling). Destroy and seal off nests and breeding places. 5. Remove all unnecessary clutter. 6. An updated sanitation plan from the Operator will be required which will outline pest control measures that will be taken on a daily basis.
