In Compliance - Food

Handwashing facilities are well-supplied. Hot and cold running water, soap and paper towel available. Refrigeration temperature(s) are satisfactory (< 4 degrees C / 40 degrees F). Freezer temperature(s) are satisfactory (< -18 degrees C / 0 degrees F). General sanitation of premises is satisfactory. No signs of pests observed at time of inspection. Pest control program in place. FoodSafe (or equivalent) requirements have been met. Food handling practices appear to be satisfactory. Temperature records up to date and available upon request. Sanitation of meat slicer appears to be satisfactory.

 0 0

Inadequate Cooling and/or Refrigerated Storage of Potentially Hazardous Food(s)

Some foods (including cooked slices of meat, prawns and cut vegetables) in the counter-top coolers were measured to be at 10 degrees C using a probe thermometer - operator stated it was in preparation for the lunch rush. Advised the operator to use ice, cover the foods with tight-fitting lids/covers or to store a smaller amount prior and after the lunch rush.

All potentially hazardous foods are to be stored in a working cooler maintained at 4 degrees C or less to slow down the growth of pathogenic bacteria.

 1 0

Inadequate Hot Holding of Potentially Hazardous Food(s)

All potentially hazardous foods are to be stored in a working hot-holding unit maintained at 60 degrees C or less to prevent the growth of pathogenic bacteria.

Food in the hot-holding unit found to be maintained at 55 degrees C - corrected at time of inspection.

Improper Cleaning, Sanitizing of Equipment and Utensils

Sanitizing solution (1 oz. bleach in 1 gallon water) is to be prepared fresh daily and located in a convenient area for use whenever necessary.

No sanitizing solution was available for use at time of inspection - operator stated the person responsible for cleaning and sanitizing was about to start their shift.

Food Not Protected - General

Bag of sugar stored directly on floor in the back kitchen by the sugar and flour bins.

Foods are to be stored off the floor (at least 6 inches) to prevent contamination and facilitate ease of cleaning and sanitizing (1 oz. bleach in 1 gallon water) of surfaces.

Food Not Protected - General

Food Not Protected - General

Various foods and sauces in the under-counter coolers are found to be uncovered. Plastic bags are found to be used to store small pieces of fried chicken in the under-counter cooler across from the prep table in the back kitchen. Bowls of uncovered food found to be double-stacked in the under-counter cooler beside the pop machine.

Foods are to be stored using food-grade containers and covered with proper tight-fitting lids or covers and not to be double-stacked to prevent contamination.
