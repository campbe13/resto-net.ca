Food Not Protected From Contamination - Other Sources

Observed water dripping from the condenser unit in the undercounter cooler. Vacuum packaged hotdogs were stored in the under counter cooler at the time of inspection. The hot dog packages were sitting directly on the bottom surface that was accumulating water. Instructed the supervisor to clean and sanitize all the packaging and place into a metal or plastic container.

> Do not store food products directly on non-potable water > Place all food products into a container to protect it from contamination > Repair the leak in the under counter cooler.

 1 0

Inadequate Cooling and/or Refrigerated Storage of Potentially Hazardous Food(s)

Observed peppers, onions and saurkrat stored in plastic containers with self serving tongs placed in an insulated ice well box on the service counter. The condiment containers did not have any lids to protect the products from contamination. Instructed the supervisor to provide lids for each container and to implement a date and time loging system to ensure that the condiments are not stored at room temperature for more than 2 hours.

> Potentially hazardous foods must not be stored at room temperature for more than 2 hours. > Place a sticker with the date and time at which the condiments must be discarded/used by (2 Hour Maximum) > Record batches on a condiment log sheet > Place lids on each container > Notify the health department for any critcal food handling changes prior to implementation

 1 0

Inadequate Cooling and/or Refrigerated Storage of Potentially Hazardous Food(s)

Measured the undercounter cooler to be at 7 C. Temperature log sheets were unavailable at the time of inspection. The manager will be monitoring the temperature within the next hour to see if the temperature falls back to 4 C or colder. Instructed the supervisor to place the majority of the pre-made salad containers into the walk-in cooler. The cheese curds were not individually portioned at the time of inspection. The staff were too busy due to the Christmas rush. The supervisor instructed an employee to portion the cheese into plastic bags. A new under counter cooler was delivered and is stored in the back. The manager will be replacing the under counter cooler after closing and will monitor the temperature prior to placing food items in the cooler for storage.

> Do not store more than a few containers of salad and bags of cheese curds in the under counter cooler until it is capable of maintaining a temperature of 4 C or colder. > Replace the under counter cooler as agreed and ensure that the internal temperature is maintained at 4 C or colder prior to storing food inside.
