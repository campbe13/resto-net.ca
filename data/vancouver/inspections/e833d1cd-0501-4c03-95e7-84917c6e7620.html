Food Handler Illness/Hygiene/Habits

Resolved - hand hygiene is better at this time. Food handlers All staff are to wash hands prior to putting on gloves and after taking gloves off (in addition to other times necessary to prevent contamination of foods). A proper hand wash consists of: vigorously rubbing together the surfaces of the lathered hands and exposed arms with soap and warm water for at least 20 seconds followed by a thorough rinsing with warm clean water. Dry hands with single-use paper towels and use the towel to turn off the water faucets to avoid re-contaminating hands.

 1 1

Inadequate Cooling and/or Refrigerated Storage of Potentially Hazardous Food(s)

Resolved - did not observe pre-made sushi rolls stored at ambient temperatures or sushi rice stored out on the counter at ambient temperatures. Potentially hazardous foods must be kept at <4 degrees C or >60 degrees C (not within the danger zone 4-60 degrees C) - keep all pre-made sushi and sushi rice in the cooler at <4 degrees C or hot held at >60 degrees C. - sushi rice that is cooked must be cooled down properly (i.e. from 60 C to 20 C or less within two hours and then from 20 C to 4 C or less within 4 hours). - place cooked foods (i.e. rice) in the cooler asap for rapid cooling and in containers with a depth <4 inches for adequate cooling. - for potentially hazardous foods a maximum of 2 hours in the dangerzone is permitted and but then foods must be discarded. A timing system will have to be implemented. - use a sanitized probe thermometer for checking the internal temperature of potentially hazardous foods and carry out necessary corrective actions.

 1 1

Improper Cleaning, Sanitizing of Equipment and Utensils

Outstanding - Maki Maker still appeared visibly unclean at the time of inspection (food debris caught in front panel still viisble from last week). The Operation Manual was not readily available at the time of inspection for referencing the cleaning and sanitizing frequency of this equipment. Re-usable equipment must be cleaned and sanitized after each use to prevent contaminating foods when used again. Each sushi rice batch that is added into the hopper and/or every 2hr time period should be a single use.

Dis-assemble machine and completely clean and sanitize after each use (or at least every 2 hours). Provide an instruction manual upon the follow-up appointment.
