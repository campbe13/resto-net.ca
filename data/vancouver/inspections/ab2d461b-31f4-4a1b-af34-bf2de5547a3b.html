Lack of/Inadequate Food Safety Plan

The temperatures of coolers, hot held food and chicken cooking temperatures are still not being monitored AND recorded. Staff were directed to record temperatures. Ensure that all coolers are supplied with thermometers. The chicken hot holding unit was not supplied with a proper hot holding thermometer. Cooked chickens are not being monitored for adequate cooking temperature.

Monitor AND record temperatures daily. This is an important part of a Food Safety Plan, which is required by law to be in place. The business is required by law to have an up to date Food Safety Plan that details how food is safely handled from receipt to sale or consumption. This plan must include the identification of critical control points (CCPs) and critical control limits (CCLs) which must be monitored daily and documented. The plan must describe the process followed to ensure adherence to the critical limits, and the actions to be taken in the event that the critical limits are not adhered to. The Food Premises Regulation (B.C. Reg. 210/99) section 23 requires that: "every operator of a food service establishment must have written procedures to ensure that a health hazard does not occur in the operation of the establishment."

Food Handler Illness/Hygiene/Habits

Staff were not observed to put on used food handling gloves. Remember that when gloves are removed, they must be discarded immediately and not used again. After gloves are removed, hands must be properly washed before new gloves are donned.

Inadequate Hot Holding of Potentially Hazardous Food(s)

Potentially hazardous foods being held hot must be held at 60 C or greater to prevent bacterial growth. The temperature of hot foods must be monitored with a sanitized probe style thermometer. Ensure compliance with section 14 of the Food Premises Regulation (B.C. Reg. 210/99): 14 (1) Every operator of food premises that processes food must ensure that the food is processed in a manner that makes it safe to eat. (2) Every operator of food premises must ensure that potentially hazardous food is stored or displayed at a temperature of not more than 4�C or not less than 60�C.

Food held in the hot holding case was measured to range from 55 - 65 C depending on the location inside the case. One heat lamp was observed to be burnt out and requires replacement. This case does not have a proper hot holding thermometer.

Improper Cleaning, Sanitizing of Equipment and Utensils

Operator must ensure compliance with Section 17 of the B.C. Food Premises Regulations: 17 (1) Every operator of food premises must ensure that the premises and the equipment and utensils used on the premises are maintained in a sanitary condition. (2) Without limiting subsection (1), every operator of food premises must ensure that the equipment, utensils and food contact surfaces used on the premises are washed and sanitized in a manner that removes contamination.

A bleach and water sanitising solution was available in a container in the sink, it seems that dishes are not being soaked in a sanitizing solution after washing and rinsing. After washing and rinsing dishes, they must then be sanitized by soaking items fully immersed in an approved sanitizing solution for two minutes before the items are removed and air dried. >To prepare a 100 ppm bleach sanitizing solution add 1/2 teaspoon of bleach to every 1 liter of water used (or two tsp of bleach per gallon of water used). Used wiping cloths were not being held in sanitizing solution. Hold wiping cloths fully immersed in a bucket of sanitizing solution when not being actively used.
