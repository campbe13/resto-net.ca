Inspection Details

Inspection Date: 08-Sep-2011
Inspector:
Follow-up Required: False
Reason for Inspection: Re-inspection
Action Taken: Written Directive, Warning Letter Issued
Referrals:
General Comments: Manager in charge present at time of inspection. Warning letter issued - see closing comments below.
Closing Comments: Vancouver Coastal Health operates a progressive enforcement policy. Continued or repeated contraventions of the BC Food Premises Regulation is subject to service of violation ticket(s) and/or closure order. A further follow up inspection will be carried out in due course at which time it is expected that all outstanding work will have been completed.

Inspection Information

Observations # of New Observations # of Resolved Observations

Inadequate Hot Holding of Potentially Hazardous Food(s)

Pizzas THE FOLLOWING CONTRAVENTION HAS NOT BEEN RESOLVED - At the time of the inspection pizzas were being displayed at room temperature and the heated display equipment for pizzas had been switched off. Pizzas must be displayed after cooking at +60C or hotter to prevent bacteria growth or a system provided using timers and times recorded when pizzas go on display. Unsold pizzas must be discarded after two hours. Clear and accurate records must be kept for this system and available for inspection when asked. I recommend that you use your heated cabinet for displaying pizzas. - pizzas were being timed to ensure a maximum display of two hours but the Operator is now selling breads containing filling of cheese and others contain beef. These were at room temperature and were not being monitored for time/temperature control. These were instructed to be handled in the same way as the pizzas.

Inadequate Insect/Rodent Control

Rear Door THE FOLLOWING CONTRAVENTION HAS NOW BEEN RESOLVED - At the time of the inspection the rear door was open. A pest screen is installed but is difficult to use. This should now be repaired and the screen is used when the back door is open for reason of ventilation.

Improper Construction/Maintenance/Sanitation of Establishment

Cleaning THE FOLLOWING CONTRAVENTION HAS NOW BEEN RESOLVED - Whilst cleaning has improved, further attention is still required in the more difficult to reach areas especially beneath work equipment, shelves and the walk-in cooler to the floor and wall junction.

Food Handler Training

Food Safety Training THE FOLLOWING CONTRAVENTION HAS NOT BEEN RESOLVED - At the time of the inspection one person was available at the facility, they did had have adequate food safety training. At all times the facility is open at least one person who has achieved Foodsafe Level I or equivalent must be at the facility. THIS IS A REPEAT VIOLATION - training to satisfy your operation needs should now be arranged.

Other - Misc

Wash Hand Station THE FOLLOWING CONTRAVENTION HAS NOW BEEN RESOLVED - At the time of the inspection a table with a double pan deep fat fryer was obstructing the wash hand sink in the rear kitchen. Access to the wash hand sink must be available at all times. THIS IS A REPEAT VIOLATION Deep Fat Fryer THE FOLLOWING CONTRAVENTION HAS NOW BEEN RESOLVED - A deep fat fryer appeared to have been in use at the facility. To operate a deep fat fryer a permit to produce grease laden vapours must be approved. You should not use the deep fat fryer without further approval from this authority.

Mop Sink THE FOLLOWING CONTRAVENTION HAS NOW BEEN RESOLVED - It is understood that the mop sink is not used and at the time of the inspection clean washing up was being stored on a rack resting on the dirty mop sink. The mop sink should be unobstructed at all times and used for the disposing of mop water. THIS IS A REPEAT VIOLATION.
