Lack of/Inadequate Food Safety Plan

Cooler temperature log records are not up to date (stopped in October). Daily temperature records are required to ensure that refrigerators are operating below the 4C / 40F standard to prevent the growth of pathogenic organisms

You must ensure that all cooler [and hot holding] temperatures are monitored twice daily and that results are recorded on paper, and kept for a period of up to 6 months. These records should be made available to the health inspector upon request.

 1 0

Inadequate Cooling and/or Refrigerated Storage of Potentially Hazardous Food(s)

Observed a bucket of cooked chicken (quarters) cooling at room temperature. Content was still hot. Moved bucket to an ice bath to speed up cooliing.

>You must ensure that all hot foods which are to be cooled down, must be cooled to below 20C within 2 hours and then below 4C within a further 4 hours. >To facilitate fast cooling, divide content into smaller portions, on a shallow pan or in an ice bath.

 1 0

Inadequate Insect/Rodent Control

Observed mouse dropping on floors along wall and corners in dry storage room. Operator advised pest control company has noticed the same problem, but pest control inspection record was produced.

1. Remove all mouse droppings from the indicated area. Thoroughly clean and sanitize area with 100ppm chlorine solution (to make add 1/2 teaspoon bleach to 1 litre of water) 2. Check premises and seal all openings (>1/4") to prevent entry of pest in premises. 3. Check premises for mouse droppings daily to monitor activity level and clean up as needed. 4. Communnicate with pest control company regarding remediation actions and ask them to provide a written report after each inspection on site.

 1 0

Inadequate Insect/Rodent Control

The rear door is a common point of access for rodent and insect pests. If left open for long periods the doorway should be fitted with a close fitting self closing pest screen door.

Rear door was open at time of inspection. Operator advised that this door is open when the kitchen gets too hot.
