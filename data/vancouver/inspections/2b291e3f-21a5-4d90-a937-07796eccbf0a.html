Food Not Protected From Contamination - Microbiological

> Reorganize the reach-in cooler and the walk-in cooler to ensure that all foods are protected from contamination > Store raw meats on the lower shelves or vertically separate from ready-to-eat foods Section 12 (a) and (b) of the Food Premises Regulation states that "Every operator of food premises must ensure that all food on the premises is protected from contamination, and stored, handled, prepared, displayed and dispensed in a sanitary manner." Failure to comply with the FPR [B.C. Reg 210/99] may result in the issuance of one or more violation tickets, having your permit revoked, closure, and/or court action.

FINAL WARNING, REPEAT INFRACTION: This is the third time raw meat has been noted to be stored above ready-to-eat foods in the cooler. All raw meats must be stored on the bottom shelving or can be separated vertically (i.e. raw meats on one side of the cooler and other non-potentially hazardous foods on the other side of the cooler)

Improper Cleaning, Sanitizing of Equipment and Utensils

> A minimum concentration of 50 ppm chlorine must be dispensed into the dishwasher for each load. > The dishwasher operator must test the dishwasher prior to each shift to verfiy that the dishwasher is working properly. > Mark the chemical containers one inch from the bottom with a black marker and indicate that the container must be changed once the chemical level reaches that line. Section 17 (1) and (2) of the Food Premises Regulation states that "Every operator of food premises must ensure that the premises and the equipment and utensils used on the premises are maintained in a sanitary condition." and "Without limiting subsection (1), every operator of food premises must ensure that the equipment, utensils and food contact surfaces used on the premises are washed and sanitized in a manner that removes contamination." Failure to comply with the FPR [B.C. Reg 210/99] may result in the issuance of one or more violation tickets, having your permit revoked, closure, and/or court action.

FINAL WARNING, REPEAT INFRACTION: Measured 0ppm chlorine at the plate level in the dishwasher. Observed half a container of chlorine available, however sanitizer solution was not being dispensed into the machine. Demonstrated how to prime the dishwasher to remove all the air in the line. Instructed the operator to share this information to the other managers and individuals who operate the dishwasher. The chlorine test strips were on a shelf that was too high for the dishwasher to reach. Explained that if the dishwasher cannot obtained the test strips then the machine is not being tested on a daily basis. Re-measured the dishwasher to contain 100ppm chlorine at the plate level.

Food Not Protected - General

> DO NOT Thaw food at room temperature. > Thaw under running cold water or in the cooler.

WARNING, REPEAT INFRACTION: Observed two large containers of raw beef thawing at room temperature on the shelving unit near the back door. Measured meat to be at 6 C. Instructed the operator to place the two containers of meat into the cooler immediately. Thawing foods at room temperature will allow microorganisms to grow. The food must not be kept in the danger zone between 4 degrees Celsius and 60 degrees Celsius for an unknown period of time.
