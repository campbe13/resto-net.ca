Inadequate Cooling and/or Refrigerated Storage of Potentially Hazardous Food(s)

Ensure that foods are cooled in a manner to prevent the rapid growth of pathogenic microorganisms. Foods must be cooled: From Hot (above 60C) to 20C within 2 hours From 20C to below 4C within 4 hours. Cool foods rapidly by using shallow containers. Place the containers into ice baths and stir frequently. Ensure that foods are measured to be below 20C before placing them into the cooler.

Observed a large container of soup cooling in the walk-in cooler. The temperature of this soup was greater than 26C at the surface. Three containers of red cabbage in the cooler.were measured to be above 30C. This is not a safe method for cooling potentially hazardous foods. Foods must cool quickly, moving through the Danger Zone (60-4C) within a very short period of time. These containers were removed from the cooler and placed in an ice bath. Staff will monitor the temperature using a thermometer and track it on the cooling log sheet.
