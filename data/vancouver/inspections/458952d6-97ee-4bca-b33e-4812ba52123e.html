In Compliance - Food

Ensure copies of staff FoodSafe Certificates are filed and available on site, and ready for health inspection up on request.

Refrigeration temperatures are satisfactory (< 4 degrees C / 40 degrees F).

Freezer temperatures are satisfactory (< -18 degrees C / 0 degrees F).

Mechanical dishwashing procedure is satisfactory (final rinse water temperature is > 71 degrees C / 160 degrees F for 10 seconds, measured at the dish; or >82 degrees C/179 degrees F, measured at the manifold).

Lack of/Inadequate Food Safety Plan

Up-to-date temperature records for coolers and hot holding units were not available. Daily temperature records are required to ensure that refrigerators are operating below the 4C / 40F and hot holding units are above 60C/140F to prevent the growth of pathogenic organisms

> Ensure that all cooler [and hot holding] temperatures are monitored twice daily and that results are recorded on paper, and that records are kept for a period of up to 6 months. Use attached logsheet as a template. > These records should be made available to the health inspector upon request.

Improper Cleaning, Sanitizing of Equipment and Utensils

1. Quat sanitizer for the bar area was tested to be 500ppm. This concentration was too high. The recommended concentration for most quat sanitizers is 200ppm. 2. Quat test strip was not available to monitor concentration of sanitizer solution after it is made.

> Ensure to follow manufacturer's instruction to achieve the correct concentration when making the solution. > Obtain Quat test strip from chemical supplier and test sanitizer concentration daily after a fresh bottle is mixed.
