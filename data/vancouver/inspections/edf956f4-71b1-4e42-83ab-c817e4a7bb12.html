Food Not Protected From Contamination - Microbiological

This will prevent raw meat blood and drippage from contaminating other foods. Provided instructional signage for the Operator. Improper Cleaning, Sanitizing of Equipment and Utensils

The cleaning/sanitizing process involves: i) removing gross debris from surfaces; ii) applying a detergent solution to loosen soil and bacterial film and hold them in solution and suspension; iii) rinsing with water to remove loosened soil and residues of detergent; iv) once cleaned, the food contact surfaces of equipment and utensils should then be sanitized by heat or chemical means. Chemical means consists of: at least 2 minutes in a chlorine solution of > 100ppm diluted bleach and water at 45 C; or at least 2 minutes in a quaternary ammonium compound solution > 200mg/L in 45 degree C water. v) Follow with an air dry.

Food contact surfaces not washed, rinsed and sanitized properly. Operator applied sanitizer spray to surfaces with raw blood spillage without washing first. Bleach was sprayed on shelving overtop uncovered open food.
