Improper Cleaning, Sanitizing of Equipment and Utensils

Inadequate amount of approved sanitizer. Chemicals on hand but no prepared sanitizer was available (chemicals in their concentrated form).

Ensure that a prepared approved sanitizer is available at all times to have on hand to use to prevent contamination of foods and to maintain the premise in a sanitary manner. An approved sanitizer consists of 100ppm chlorine (1 tsp of bleach per gallon of water) or 200ppm quaternary ammonium (mix according to manufactures instructions). Use test strips to check the concentration for adequacy and refresh as needed.

 1 0

Inadequate Insect/Rodent Control

Evidence of rodent activity observed. Noted droppings in various areas of the premise (i.e. behind upright cooler in back, behind chest freezer, in cabinet storing chemicals, near the back door next to the trap). Building is recently undergoing renovations to the washrooms outside. These could be recent droppings or old droppings that were not cleaned up at the start of the season. Food debris noted in hard to reach areas.

The presence of rodents or insects must be treated immediately by inspecting and discarding any adulterated food. The inspection must be followed by: i) removing dirt, soil or filth ii) cleaning and sanitizing surfaces contaminated by pests - ensure that all food contact surfaces are sanitized before the start of the day and throughout the day. iii) destroying and sealing off nests and breeding places; and iv) protecting the food premises against the entrance of pests - seal all entrance holes and cracks. v) have pest control set up more traps in areas where droppings or sightings are noted for better monitoring and control
