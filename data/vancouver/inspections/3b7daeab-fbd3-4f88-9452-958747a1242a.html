Lack of/Inadequate Food Safety Plan

It is a requirement under the BC Food Premises Regulation section 23(2) to have a food safety plan addressing food handling procedures: "Every operator of a food service establishment must have written procedures to ensure that a health hazard does not occur in the operation of the establishment." Failure to comply will result in ticketing of $230. Submit a written draft of your food safety plan during re-inspection.

OUTSTANDING: No food safety plan in place. Repeat observations on poor food handling practices. In your food safety plan, you must address the following issues: -what is adequate refrigeration temperatures and how to monitor these temperatures. -what is adequate hot-holding temperatures in oven and how to monitor these temperatures. -how to defrost/ thaw foods properly and at what temperatures -where and how (procedures) to cool down hot foods properly -how to store raw meats/seafoods/ready-to-eat foods/vegetables in the cooler -how to store dry goods properly You should also address who is in charge to ensure temperature monitoring and recording of all coolers/ hot-holding units are completed on a daily basis. Translate your foodsafety plan to all staff and food handlers to ensure proper food safety and food handling procedures are in compliance. Ultimately it is the owner's responsibility to ensure safe food handling.

Food Not Protected From Contamination - Microbiological

Violation ticket issued for failure to protect food from contamination and handle food in sanitary manner. Repeat violation: Buckets or containers of raw meats stored above vegetables in sliding door coolers. Other poor food handling practices observed during time of inspection: -Blocks of shrimp stored under sink defrosting at room temperature -2 boxes of shrimp stored at room temperature -Spare ribs in plastic bags defrosting in pot of water with spices measured 17 degrees C. -Poultry hung above pot of jelly fish with meat juices dripping into pot.

CORRECTIVE ACTIONS: Ensure raw meats/ seafood are stored below vegetables and ready-to-eat food. OR store raw meats and vegetables on separate shelving.
