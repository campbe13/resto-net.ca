Inadequate Cooling and/or Refrigerated Storage of Potentially Hazardous Food(s)

Ensure that the level of the ice is high enough to cool the yogurt. This is to be checked regularly. If is not busy, then the yogurt should be placed in the refrigerator to maintain a temperature of 4C or less.

OBSERVED: A big container of yogurt was sitting at room temperature. The ice in the cooling unit had melted to a point where it was no longer cooling the yogurt.

Inadequate Cooking and/or Reheating of Potentially Hazardous Food(s)

OBSERVED: The display cooler displayed a temperature of -0.1C however, the internal temperature a chicken sandwich was measured to be 14C. This issue with the display cooler was also noted during the inspection on May 10, 2010

Hazardous food items (the wraps) that were in the cooler were there overnight and have been discarded. Ensure that the unit is repaired so that the temperature on the display is the actual internal temperature. As per the May 12, 2010 inspection, an internal thermometer was placed in the unit however it was not during this inspection. Ensure that a thermometer in placed in the unit to measure the temperature in the event that the gauge on the machine fails again. The temperature of the refrigerator must be 4C or less Temperatures all for cooling units must be monitored and recorded daily to ensure that problems like this are caught early.

Improper Cleaning, Sanitizing of Equipment and Utensils

OBSERVED: Dishes are not being sanitized after being washed. Was informed that the staff are developing extremely dry skin with a dermatitis appearence and owner believes it may be the dish soap or the sanitizer

Contacted and discussed the sanitizing procedure with Saron Stewart, District Manager at Booster Juice who informed that the chemical company of the issue. The company will be coming to evaluate the cleaning and disenfecting procedure for the dishes. To prevent dry hands and irritated skin, dish gloves will be worn from now on as per Booster Juice's requirements. All dishes must be properly washed, rinsed and sanitized to prevent bacterial and mold growth.
