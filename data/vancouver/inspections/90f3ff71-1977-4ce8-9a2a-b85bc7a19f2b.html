Food Not Protected - General

1. Observed drink containers being stored in the same ice as for beverages in the bar area. 2. Ice scoop for ice machine stored on top of the ice machine. 3. Foods in walk-in cooler un-covered. 4. Foods in open bags (i.e. chest nuts) stored in basement area un-covered. The basement area is not pest-proofed.

1. Refrain from storing containers in the same ice used for food (i.e. drinks) to protect from contamination. 2. Store ice scoop utensil in a seperate container to protect utensil from contamination. Wash, rinse, sanitize equipment regularily. 3. Cover all foods with protective lids to protect from contamination. 4. Store foods in appropriate food storage areas (basement area is not ideal since it is dusty and not pest proof) and all open containers of food in protective containers with tight-fitting lids.

Improper Sanitary Facilities

Staff use the 2-comp sink in the kitchen area for handwashing however, this sink does not have a faucet that will allow running hot/cold water (it is only equipped with a spray nozzle). Note: There is a small hand washing sink around the corner in the pass thru. Providing this equipment (proper handwashing facilities) ensures foodhandlers are able to properly wash their hands to prevent contamination of foods. Handwashing is the single most effective method to prevent the transmission of any disease.

If staff are using the 2-comp sink for hand washing, ideally the sink should be equipped with a faucet that allows running hot and cold water for adequate lathering and rinsing of hands. Please equip the 2-comp sink with a faucet in addition to the spray nozzle.

Inadequate Insect/Rodent Control

The presence of rodents or insects must be treated immediately by: 1. Inspecting and discard any adulterated food. Store all foods in pest proof containers. 2. Removing dirt, soil or filth if present and verify cleaning procedures. 3. Inspecting areas of the premise for pest activity (DAILY) and clean and sanitize surfaces contaminated by pests (i.e. by using 1000ppm diluted bleach wearing gloves and a mask). 4. Destroying and sealing off nests and breeding places. 5. Removing all unnecessary clutter. 6. Protecting the food premises against the entrance of pests - seal all gaps and holes.

Rodent droppings observed in the basement storage room. Premise is on a contract with a pest control company.
