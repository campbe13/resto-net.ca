Food Not Protected From Contamination - Microbiological

Keep all raw meat separately from reat-to-eat food items, garnish, baked goods and other non hazards food items. Store them all on their own rack or below the ready-to-eat items. Re-organize all of the cooling units to ensure that this storage method is employed. Section 12 of Food Premises Regulation states that "Every operator of food premises must ensure that all food on the premises is protected from contamination and stored, hanlded, prepared, displayed and dispensed in a sanitary manner.

Observed impoper storage of cooling units. Raw meat was stored over read-to-eat food items. This has also been noted during the April 6, 2011 Inspection.

Inadequate Cooling and/or Refrigerated Storage of Potentially Hazardous Food(s)

Observed whole cooked duck hanging over prep sink at room temperature The duck was at 10C. Operator immediately started to take down the duck and placed it in the refrigerator upon the Health Inspector's arrival. The operator claimed the the duck had only been made and hanging to cool for 30 minutes

Ensure that proper cooling methods are employed for the duck. Food items must be cooled from 60C to 20C within 2 hours and 20C to 4C within 4 hours. To cool it quickly, place the duck in a shallow pan and place that pan in an ice water bath or place the duck into the refrigerator after 1 hour if the refrigerator is capable of handling the heat. This s the second time this has been observed. Food Premises Regulation Section 14(2) states that Every operator of food premises must ensure that potentially hazardous food is stored or displayed at a temperature of not more than 4 C or not less than 60 C. Further non compliance may result in food being discarded and or issuance of a $345 violation ticket.

 1 0

Improper Cleaning, Sanitizing of Equipment and Utensils

The premise needs to be thoroughly cleaned. There was a build-up of dirt and old food on the floor and in corners and encrusted food on surfaces

Thoroughly clean the premise. Sweep and mop the floors, clean all surfaces and spills, scrape any encrusted food or grease. It is important for safe food handling to make food in a kitchen that is clean. Thoroughly clean the holder for the cleavers and ensure that they are always cleaned regularly. Remove all towels used as liners. Surfaces must be non-porous and easy to clean.

 1 0

Improper Cleaning, Sanitizing of Equipment and Utensils

Observed sanitizer for surfaces not present in the premise. Dirty wiping cloths were stored everywhere at room temperature: on the counters, on top of equipment, near the sink

All wiping cloths must be stored in a sanitizer to prevent bacterial growth. During the inspection, a working 200ppm concentration of bleach sanitizer was made with 1tsp of bleach to 1 litre of water and all wiping cloths were stored in it. make this solution daily at the beginning of the work day and change the sanitizer regularly when it gets dirty. Ensure that when cloths get too dirty, they are replaced with clean cloths.

 1 0

Lack of/Inadequate Sanitation Plan

A sanitation plan is not available for the premise

Create a cleaning schedule for the premise. This will help in ensuring that ti is being cleaned regularly. The schedule must include items to be cleaned daily, weekly and nightly. Items cleaned should be verified that they have been cleaned.

Improper Construction/Maintenance/Sanitation of Establishment

Observed lights with missing covers and a build-up of dust that was being blown by the kitchen fan.

Lights must be covered so that in the event they break, glass will not get into food items. As well, the covering will prevent dust build-up around them that can fall into food. Clean the light fixture and place a cover over the fixture or a cover over the actual light bulb.
