In Compliance - Food

Foodsafe or equivalent training requirement has been satisfied.

Food handling practices appear to be satisfactory.

Refrigeration temperatures are satisfactory (< 4 degrees C / 40 degrees F).

Freezer temperatures are satisfactory (< -18 degrees C / 0 degrees F).

Hot holding temperatures are satisfactory (> 60 degrees C / 140 degrees F).

Mechanical dishwashing procedure is satisfactory (final rinse water temperature is > 71 degrees C / 160 degrees F for 10 seconds, measured at the dish; or >82 degrees C/179 degrees F, measured at the manifold).

Mechanical glass washing procedure is satisfactory (final rinse iodine concentration is between 12.5 ppm and 25 ppm in rinse water >24 degrees C/75 degrees F).

Pest control program in place.

Sanitation of premises is satisfactory.

Thermometers are provided in every cooling unit containing perishable food.

 0 0

Lack of/Inadequate Food Safety Plan

Ensure that temperatures of ALL refrigerated and freezer storage units are being monitoring and documented at least once a day as assurance that the food safety plan is being adhered to and that potentially hazardous foods are being stored at safe temperatures at all times.

Temperature records of coolers and freezers were not kept up-to-date at the time of the inspection. Note: Will forward templates to the Manager to be used in the kitchen.

 1 0

Food Handler Illness/Hygiene/Habits

Ensure that knives are properly cleaned and sanitized on a regular basis and that they are stored in a manner that prevents potential contamination. The spacing between the butcher block and the steel prep-table can be not easily monitored and cleaned therefore, if knives are to be placed there, the blade can potentially become contaminated.

Knives were stored between the butcher block and a stainless steel table. Knives were observed to be greasy and the blade contained food debris. This was brought to the attention of the Manager and was corrected at the time of the inspection.

 1 1

Improper Cleaning, Sanitizing of Equipment and Utensils

Sanitizing solution [100 ppm bleach (1 oz. bleach per 1 gallon of water)] was not available but was corrected at the time of the inspection.

Ensure that kitchen staff have access to 100 ppm (1 oz. bleach per 1 gallon of water) of sanitizer solution and that unused towels are submerged in the sanitizer solution to facilitate proper sanitizing of food contact surfaces. Do not mix detergent into the sanitizer solution as this will prevent the chlorine from dissipating.

 1 1

Food Not Protected - General

Various bins were double stacked in the walk-in cooler and in the prep-cooler. This issue was corrected at the time of the inspection. Light covers are missing for three of the light fixtures in the kitchen. i) above dim-sum cooking area ii) above deep fryer area iii) above the stainless steel table next to the dim-sum cooking area Fan grill in the slim walk-in freezer was accumulated in dirt and debris. Fan grill in the walk-in cooler was accumulated with dirt and debris. A Table cloth was used to shield vegetables from the frigid cold in the walk-in cooler to prevent cold damage. However, the cloth was observed to have major spots of mould. Fortunately, non of the foods were in direct contact with the mould. Nevertheless, this was corrected at the time of the inspection.

Ensure that if food items are to be double-stacked for storage, proper food-grade covers or saran wrap is used to prevent food from being in direct contact with the bottom of the bins. Failure to do so will contaminate the food. Ensure that all lights in the kitchen area are properly fitted with a safety shield. In case of accidental breakage, the shield will prevent glass shards from contaminating the food and injuring customers. Ensure that fan grills accumulated in dirt and debris are dismantled from its mechanical storage unit and properly cleaned and sanitized to prevent the potential contamination of food. Ensure that table clothes are not used to protect food in the cooler as it has the ability to absorb moisture which is likely why spots of mould was observed on the cloth. Mould has been associated with adverse health effects.

Food Not Protected - General

A pile of flower and salt in bags were stored on a wooden board directly on the floor in the dry storage room

Ensure that foods are stored on a shelf that is high enough to facilitate proper cleaning and sanitizing of the floors on a regular basis and to allow for proper pest monitoring.

 1 0

Improper Sanitary Facilities

Ensure that paper towels are available to all staff members at all times to facilitate proper handwashing in addition to hot and cold running water, and dispensable liquid soap.

Multiple hand-washing sinks were not adequate for proper handwashing at the time of the inspection. i) Paper towel dispenser was empty at the 3 compartment sink in the back of the kitchen by the dry storage area. ii) Paper towel dispenser was jammed in the right corner facing the entrance of the kitchen. iii) Paper towel dispenser was jammed in the staff washroom. iv) Paper towel dispenser was empty at the bar area. All of the above were corrected at the time of the inspection.

 4 4

Inadequate Insect/Rodent Control

Ensure that all areas of the kitchen, especially the floors and food contact surfaces are continually being properly cleaned and sanitized to prevent the accumulate of grease and moisture to reduce the risk of pest infestations. Ensure that food contact surfaces and floors are being sanitized with 100 ppm of bleach solution (1 oz. bleach per 1 gallon of water) to effectively destroy any pathogens that may be transmitted with the cockroaches. Ensure that the cracks noted by the pest control technician are properly sealed as this may be how the cockroaches are entering the establishment.

Dead cockroaches were observed on sticky traps behind the upright freezers. This was brought to the attention of the pest control technician who appeared on-site at the end of the inspection. Note that general sanitation was adequate at the time of the inspection.

 1 0

Improper Construction/Maintenance/Sanitation of Establishment

Ensure that the two freezers are properly defrosted as it will improve storage conditions and allow for better cleaning and sanitizing of the shelves. Ensure that the gap is filled to prevent the entry of pests such as mice which can potentially contaminate food contact surfaces and to food itself.

The two upright freezers were accumulated with frost along all surfaces on the inside. A small gap was observed at the base by the back door leading to the exterior in the dry storage room.

 3 0

Improper Construction/Maintenance/Sanitation of Establishment

Ensure that ceiling tiles remain in place to prevent the potential of falling debris or dirt from above which can potentially contaminate food and/or food contact surfaces. Ensure that the leak below the 3 compartment sink is properly taken care of to prevent the accumulation of drainage which can result in unsanitary conditions. Ensure that when time is available, remove the broken deep fryer and the microwave to free up space to improve cleaning and sanitizing and proper pest monitoring.

Ceiling tile was displaced to increase air flow in the dim-sum cooking area. This was corrected at the time of the inspection. Small leak was observed below the 3 compartment sink by the dry storage area. Manager noted that this can be correct this right away. Broken and unused deep fryer was kept in the kitchen beside the two working deep fryers. Manager noted that the deep fryer will be properly disposed off. Broken microwave was kept in the dry storage area. Manager noted that the microwave will be properly disposed off as well.
