Inspection Details

Inspection Date: 01-Apr-2011
Inspector:
Follow-up Required: False
Reason for Inspection: Routine
Action Taken: Verbal Directive, Written Directive
Referrals:
General Comments: Discussed inspection report with David Speight. Foods, placed inside the ice wells, must be further submerged into the ice. Product was merely resting on top of the ice, which is not conducive to appropriate temperature control. This applied to the kitchen, as well as in the action stations. This applies to the "seafood boat". Foods were placed on top of this display and not maintaining the appropriate temperatures. Foods, placed under the heat lamps, were not appropriately stored under the lamps and maintaining an internal temperature of 60 C and greater.

Observations # of New Observations # of Resolved Observations

In Compliance - Food

High temperature dishwasher was tested at 77.6 C, at the final rinse cycle. Quaternary ammonium sanitizer was tested and recorded at 200 ppm.

Food Safety Plan has been implemented and is in use.

Sanitation Plan has been implemented and is in use.

Refrigeration temperatures are satisfactory (< 4 degrees C / 40 degrees F).

Freezer temperatures are satisfactory (< -18 degrees C / 0 degrees F).

Mechanical dishwashing procedure is satisfactory (final rinse water temperature is > 71 degrees C / 160 degrees F for 10 seconds, measured at the dish; or >82 degrees C/179 degrees F, measured at the manifold).

Handwashing facilities are satisfactory.

Pest control program in place.

 0 0

Lack of/Inadequate Food Safety Plan

With respect to the action stations, it is recommended to maintain time logs. This log will state the time the product was processed and placed on the station and removed.

 1 0

Food Not Protected From Contamination - Microbiological

All foods must be covered with sneeze guards. Due to the sheer volume of the foods, it is important that appropriate storage methods are utilized. Recommend obtaining more plexi-glass covers or saran wrap individual food items.

An insufficient number of sneeze guards were used at the action stations. It is best to cover all foods at all times of the services.

 1 0

Inadequate Cooling and/or Refrigerated Storage of Potentially Hazardous Food(s)

Foods stored in the ice wells, were found on top of the ice.

When ice wells are used for storage of potentially hazardous foods, it is important that the foods are fully submerged to the lip of the insert. This will allow for improved temperature control and provides a minimum internal temperature of 4 C and below. The seafood boat, holding prawns, was stored on top of the ice. This is not an appropriate method of storage. Recommend either placing ice chips throughout the food product. Chef mentioned that the boat is stored in the cooler prior to use. However, this is a higher risk food item, it is best to meet the minimum temperature of 4 C and below.

 1 0

Inadequate Cooling and/or Refrigerated Storage of Potentially Hazardous Food(s)

Milk cooler was tested at 10 C. The temperature logs indicated that the cooler was originally at 4 C and below. Recommend removing the build-up of ice at the top of the cooler. Maintain the internal temperature of the food product at 4 C and below.

 1 0

Inadequate Hot Holding of Potentially Hazardous Food(s)

Heat lamps were used for hot holding, action stations. Foods must attain an internal temperature of 60 C and greater. If there is not a sufficient supply of heat lamps, the food product must be stored in hot boxes or at 4 C and below, within the cooler. In order to meet the demand of the operation, there must be adequate number of lamps to attain 60 C and greater.

 1 0

Improper Cleaning, Sanitizing of Equipment and Utensils

Reduce the concentration of the iodine in the glasswasher. The concentration was measured at 25 ppm, minimum requirement is 12.5 ppm.

 1 0

Food Not Protected - General

Ice scoop was found inside the ice well, recommend placing the scoop either outside of the actual container OR ensure the scoop handle is found outside of the ice. Ensure food items are stored a minimum of 6" off the floor, especially in the walk-in cooler. Do not use milk crates for the storage of food products. Foods must be stored on food grade shelving units.

 2 0

Poor Sanitary Design, Construction, Installation of Equipment and Utensils

Large preparation cooler is found on-site and it not in operation. Due to the limited space and it would be best to remove this large unit and replace with a cutting station, such as stainless steel table.

 1 0

Inadequate Insect/Rodent Control

Remove all droppings. Clean and sanitize areas with a bleach-water sanitizing solution (1 part of bleach to 10 parts of water: 500 ppm). Clean kitchen and keep clean. Ensure all foods are stored in pest proof containers with tight fitting lids. Abell Pest Control conducts pest control, reports are provided to management on a weekly basis. Rodent proof the building by sealing holes with steel wool and spray foam, and keeping the back door closed at all times. Provide additional mouse traps, including snap traps, glue/ sticky boards, and tin cat (metal box) style traps.

Rodent droppings were observed in the hard to reach areas, especially near the heat exchanger, under the ice machine and in the far right corner.

 1 0

Other - Misc

Observed a leak under the three compartment sink and the steamer. Recommend fixing these leaks as soon as possible. Place a container under the leaks, in order to deter slipping hazards.
