Include critical steps such as thawing, cooking (adequate temperature), hot holding (rice held in rice cookers), cooling and reheating. 1. A written Food Safety Plan was not available on site. 2. Observed improper thawing practice indicates adequate food safety plan is not being implemented.

Lack of/Inadequate Food Safety Plan

Up-to-date temperature records for coolers and hot holding units are not available.

> Ensure that ALL COOLERS have a dedicated thermometer SECURED/FIXED inside and located in a easily readable position. > Obtain a stem thermometer to check temperature of ALL HOT HOLDING units, e.g. rice kept in rice cookers, sauces and/or soups. > Ensure temperatures of all coolers/hot holding units are monitored and recorded twice a day. Keep records on site in an organized manner for minimum 6 months. > These records should be made available to the health inspector upon request.

Food Not Protected From Contamination - Microbiological

> Ensure food is thawed in a manner that prevents rapid growth of pathogenic bacteria. > Acceptable methods are: i) under refrigeration at 4/C (40F) or less; ii) completely submerged in cold running water; iii) as part of the cooking process (but only when thawing is taken into consideration in determining cooking time); and iv) by microwaving.

Observed frozen fish filets being thawed in still water in the sink. Fish was still frozen. Operator turned on cold water for proper thawing.

 1 0

Food Not Protected From Contamination - Other Sources

Observed food kept in original metal cans after opening.

Transfer food to food grade contaniers with lids once the original metal can is open since the can won't seal properly after being opened.

 1 0

Inadequate Hot Holding of Potentially Hazardous Food(s)

Cooked rice in one rice cooker was measured to be 53C. Operator explained that rice cooker has not been working properly and will be replaced, and that the rice was left over from yesterday and heated up in microwave this morning. Operator decided to discard the rice.

Ensure cooked rice held in rice cooker must be kept at greater than 60C to prevent rapid growth of pathogenic microorganisms.

Improper Construction/Maintenance/Sanitation of Establishment

The following items were observed in the dry storage room: > build up of flour and rice on floors under shelves > several open bags of dry ingredients sitting directly on floor > many items sitting on floors instead of being stored on shelves > personal items stored amongst food items on the same shelves > lights against the back wall were not working - room is dim These conditions may attract pests by serving as food source and harborage if not corrected.

> Clean indicated areas. Include in sanitation plan that this area will be cleaned at least once a week or whenever there is a spill of flour, rice or other foods. > Store dry ingredients such as rice and flour in pest resistant containers, and containers must be securely closed when not in use. > Keep items at least 6" off flour (except for large flour bins and plastic buckets) to allow easy access for cleaning. Ensure large containers are pulled out when cleaning areas around and underneath. > Organize shelves and ensure personal items are stored distinctively separated from food items. > Repair lights. Adequate lighting is required for proper cleaning.
