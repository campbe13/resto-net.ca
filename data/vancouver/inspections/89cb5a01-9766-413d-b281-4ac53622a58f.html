Food Not Protected From Contamination - Microbiological

Meat Meat is being marinated at room temperature. Rice Stirring Cooked rice is stirred with a wooden stick and stored in water, when not in use, at room temperature. Wash Hand Sink The wash hand sink within the kitchen is being used to prepare food. Rice Noodles Large containers of cooked rice noodles are being stored at room temperature. Bean Sprouts Large containers of bean sprouts were measured at +14C. Tongs Tongs are being stored dirty at room temperature.

To prevent rapid bacteria growth, raw meat should be kept at +4C or colder at all times prior to cooking. To prevent rapid bacteria growth, the rice stirring stick should be properly washed between use or stored in ice. Wash hand sinks are for hand washing only. To prevent rapid bacteria growth, bean sprouts should be kept at +4C or colder at all times prior to cooking. To prevent rapid bacteria growth, when tongs are not in use they should be stored in ice or in hot water in excess of +60C. To prevent rapid bacteria growth, rice noodles should be kept at +4C or colder. When needed, they can be removed from the fridge for a maximum of two hours prior to reheating and serving.

 6 0

Food Handler Illness/Hygiene/Habits

Hand Washing During the inspection hand washing was not routinely being carried out after handling potentially high risk food. When hand washing was asked for by the inspector it was carried out without hot water or soap. Gloves were being worn by one member of staff but these were washed in the same way.

Hand washing must be carried out routinely and after handling potentially high risk food, such as raw meat and fish, using hot water and liquid hand soap and drying with a clean dry towel.

 1 0

Improper Cleaning, Sanitizing of Equipment and Utensils

A three step WASH, RINSE, SANITIZE procedure must now be used to ensure eating utensils are cleaned and sanitized. Both degreaser for cleaning and sanitizer for disinfecting should be readily available, in clearly labeled spay bottles for example, for cleaning and sanitizing respectively. Wiping cloths should be clean, dedicated in use and when not in use, stored in sanitizer. The food slicer should be cleaned and sanitized after use and should not be left for more than two hours between cleaning.

Washing Up A three step washing process is not being followed. Food Contact Surfaces Degreaser and sanitizer were not readily available for cleaning/disinfecting. Wiping Cloths Cloths used for wiping food contact surfaces were observed dirty and stored on food contact surfaces at room temperature. Food Slicer The food slicer is left dirty after use.

Food Not Protected - General

To prevent risk of cross contamination of food contact surfaces from the bottom of containers stored on the floor, food should not be stored on the floor.

Food On Floor Food is being stored on the floor of the walk-in cooler.

 1 0

Inadequate Insect/Rodent Control

Fly Screen The fly screen is damaged.

To prevent risk of flying insects when the door is open the screen should be repaired.

 1 0

Improper Construction/Maintenance/Sanitation of Establishment

Chest Freezers These are dirty and are not being routinely cleaned. Cleaning Generally cleaning at the facility is poor. Grease Filters The grease filters were heavily laden with grease and appeared never to be cleaned. Hood Lights The two light fittings within the exhaust ventilation system are not working.

Freezers should now be defrosted and cleaned and carried out routinely. All floors beneath and behind work equipment, walls, shelves and all work equipment should be routinely cleaned and sanitized. To prevent risk of contaminating food from dripping grease and to prevent risk of poor air quality within the kitchen environment, the grease filters should be cleaned at least once per week (or more often as necessary). For good cleaning practices and for reasons of health and safety practice, the light fittings should now be repaired.

It is unclear if or when the Manager has taken food safety training. The Manager should now retake the Foodsafe Level I training.

Food Safety Training A number of poor practices were observed at the facility during inspection.
