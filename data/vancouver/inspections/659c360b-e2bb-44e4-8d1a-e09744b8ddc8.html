Inadequate Cooling and/or Refrigerated Storage of Potentially Hazardous Food(s)

Observed a small display cooler in the front retail area storing potentially hazardous foods at unsafe temperatures, ie. deep fried glueten, mango pudding. The cooler was unplugged. >Ensure potentially hazardous foods are stored cold at 4C or colder at all times.

Inadequate Hot Holding of Potentially Hazardous Food(s)

Observed a large foil tray filled with bbq meats, labelled "Chef Selection of BBQ Meats" stored on top of steam table inserts. Food temperatures were measured with a probe thermometer: roast pork 38C, bbq pork 40C. Food was reheated at the time of the inspection. Operator indicated this food was an order for delivery and will be delivered soon. >Ensure potentially hazardous foods are stored hot at 60C or hotter at all times. Store food in the oven or steamer at 60C or hotter until the delivery occurs.

 1 0

Inadequate Hot Holding of Potentially Hazardous Food(s)

REPEAT INFRACTION: Failure to comply with the BC Food Premises Regulation may result in a violation ticket being issued, Section 14 (2) "Fail to store or display food at a safe temperature" $345.

Observed potentially hazardous foods stored in the bbq hot holding oven at unsafe temperatures less than 60C. Food temperatures were measured with a probe thermometer: 3.5 ducks 45C; 0.5 soya chicken 28C; 10 lbs bbq pork 36C; 5 lbs chinese sausage 32C; 6 lbs roast pork 35C. The operator discarded the food at the time of the inspection for time/ temperature abuse. No time/ temperature logs are being maintained. >Ensure potentially hazardous foods are stored hot at 60C or hotter at all times. Temperature logs must be completed.

 1 0

Improper Cleaning, Sanitizing of Equipment and Utensils

Observed poor level of sanitation in many areas, including but not limited to the following areas: -shelving and cutting boards in the bbq hot holding oven area. -floors throughout the premises, including under the bbq hot holding oven, the bbq kitchen area, under the potsticker grill, under the steam table area, under the wok line and other areas -door and floor in the walkin cooler -pot stove in the bbq kitchen area >Thorough cleaning required. Regular cleaning must be completed as often as necessary to maintain the premises in a sanitary condition.

Failure to comply with the BC Food Premises Regulation may result in a violation ticket being issued, Section 17: "Fail to maintain premises, equipment and utensils in sanitary condition" $230.

 1 0

Improper Cleaning, Sanitizing of Equipment and Utensils

Food contact surfaces, such as counters, butcher blocks, and cutting boards, are not washed, rinsed, and sanitized before and after each use. Operator indicated that sanitizer is only used once per day during the night cleaning. >Ensure a sanitizer solution is available at all times in each food handling area in a bucket or spray bottle for sanitizing food contact surfaces. Surfaces must be washed with detergent, rinsed, and then sanitized after each use. >Use 1 tsp. bleach in 1 litre water.

Inadequate Insect/Rodent Control

Observed a few live cockroaches on the floor under the 2-door freezer in the dishwashing area. Many dead cockroaches were observed throughout the premises, and some live cockroaches were observed on glue trpas. Large puddles of water observed under the mechancial dishwasher. Pest control measures are in place for cockroaches, however additional work must be completed. >Provide a copy of the pest control reports from the past 3 months. >Remove all cockroaches.
