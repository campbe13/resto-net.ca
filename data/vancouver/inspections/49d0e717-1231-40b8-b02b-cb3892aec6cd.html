107 C, 4390 Gallant Avenue
North Vancouver BC V7G 1L2

Primary Operator: Erick Kauko & Alistair Knox
Capacity: 82 Service Type: Restaurant - Full Service

Inspection Details

Inspection Date: 16-Mar-2011
Inspector:
Follow-up Required: False
Reason for Inspection: Routine
Action Taken: Written Directive
Referrals:
General Comments: Inspection conducted late morning. Facility has undergone some removations since last visit both in the kitchen and the dining area. Floor, walls, ceiling, lighting and ventilation all appear to have been updated. An new cooler has been installed in the dining room for wine, beer kegs which were previously in the kitchen's walk in cooler, and a small amount of items for servers accessible through a small glass door in the kitchen area. Three dry storage cupboards located in the hallway to the washrooms.
Closing Comments: The recent renovations have had a positive effect on the working environment in the kitchen and the ability to clean the kitchen. The relocation of the beer kegs does provide a bit more room in the "step in" cooler but still feel the facility would benefit from more cooling equipment in the kitchen.

Inadequate Cooling and/or Refrigerated Storage of Potentially Hazardous Food(s)

Walkin cooler and prep-line coolers operating OK but the standup glass door cooler is operating at 8c. Internal check of food items 5c Temperature logs present and notes excessive cooler temps at times but internal temp of foods was still OK.

Continue to monitor the cooler and make adjustments to ensure unit remains below 4c at all times.

Improper Cleaning, Sanitizing of Equipment and Utensils

Containers of concentrated quats sanitizer present at both food prep areas in the kitchen; both tested at 200ppm quats - good. A similar spray bottle is required for the bar area. Kitchen staff state the food prep counters are sanitized daily.

Food contact surfaces and equipment need to be cleaned and sanitized AT LEAST once every two hours and when required. Ensure the bar is provided with a sanitizer solution.

Food Not Protected - General

Cupboards for dry goods in the hallway are lockable but were unlocked at time of visit.

To prevent tampering with the food in these cupboards they should be kept locked at all times.

 0 0

Poor Sanitary Design, Construction, Installation of Equipment and Utensils

Ice is considered a food item and must be handled with the same level of sanitation as a customer's food order. Ice scoop needs to be stored in/on a food grade container/tray capable of being routinely washed and sanitized along with the ice scoop. Never store the scoop in the ice bin as the handle can potentially contaminate the ice.

Ice scoop in bar area is again laying down on top of the drink ice.

 1 0

Improper Sanitary Facilities

Both liquid handsoap and single use disposable paper handtowels in proper dispensers are required at all handsinks. Hand sanitizer is acceptable for places like the Reservation Booth but plain and simple soap is required when staff are washing their hands.

Paper hand towels lacking at bar hand sink. Hand sanitizer present in a pump dispenser but no liquid soap. Kitchen handsink also lacking paper hand towels in the immediate area of the handsink.
